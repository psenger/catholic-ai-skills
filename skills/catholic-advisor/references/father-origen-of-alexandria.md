# Origen of Alexandria (c. 185 – c. 254)

### Origen of Alexandria (c. 185 – c. 254)

**Era:** Greek (Alexandrian) | **Feast day:** none (not canonized) | **Age at death:** approximately 69
**Doctor of the Church:** No (and his theological status is irregular: some of his teachings were condemned by the Second Council of Constantinople in 553; he is nevertheless venerated as a great teacher in many Eastern traditions, and the Catholic Church preserves him as a foundational, if cautioned, voice)
**Major works:** *De Principiis* (On First Principles); *Contra Celsum* (Against Celsus); the *Hexapla* (a six-column edition of the Old Testament); numerous biblical commentaries and homilies
**Known for:** the most prolific Christian writer of antiquity; the founder of systematic scriptural exegesis; the originator of the threefold sense of Scripture (literal, moral, spiritual)

Origen's father was martyred when Origen was a teenager; Origen wanted to follow him into martyrdom but his mother hid his clothes. He spent his life teaching and writing. His output was so vast that the catalogue of his works runs to thousands of items. His exegetical method shaped every subsequent Christian commentary tradition: every layer of meaning in Scripture — historical, moral, mystical — is the fruit of the same divine Author. He was tortured under the Decian persecution and died from his injuries. After his death, certain extreme propositions associated with his name — the pre-existence of souls, the *apokatastasis* or final restoration of all rational creatures including Satan — were condemned (553). Origen himself wrote with appropriate qualification on these matters; whether the condemnations were aimed at his actual positions or at the views of later Origenists is debated. He is read today with profound respect and appropriate care.
