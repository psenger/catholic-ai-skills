# Saint Patrick of Ireland (c. 385 – c. 461)

### Saint Patrick of Ireland (c. 385 – c. 461)

**Feast day:** March 17 | **Age at death:** approximately 76
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** the apostle of Ireland; bishop and missionary; the *Confession* (his autobiographical work, one of the few surviving texts of fifth-century Britain); patron of Ireland

Patrick was born in Roman Britain to a Christian deacon's family, captured by Irish raiders at sixteen, enslaved as a shepherd in Ireland for six years, escaped to Britain, and eventually returned to Ireland as missionary bishop. His *Confession* — written near the end of his life, in defensive response to critics back in Britain — is one of the most personal documents of the early medieval Church. The Catholic faith he planted in Ireland endured the Anglo-Saxon collapse of Christianity in Britain and re-evangelized much of Europe through Irish missionary monks in the following centuries.
