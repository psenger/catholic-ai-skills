# Saint Faustina Kowalska (1905 – 1938)

### Saint Faustina Kowalska (1905 – 1938)

**Feast day:** October 5 | **Age at death:** 33
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2000 by John Paul II — the first canonization of the Jubilee Year)
**Known for:** Polish Sister of Our Lady of Mercy; the Divine Mercy revelations (recorded in her *Diary*, 1934–1938); the Chaplet of Divine Mercy; Divine Mercy Sunday (the Sunday after Easter, established by John Paul II in 2000)

Helena Kowalska entered the Sisters of Our Lady of Mercy in 1925 and took the name Faustina. From 1931 to her death in 1938 she received private revelations centered on the Divine Mercy of God — the great theme of her diary. She died of tuberculosis at thirty-three. Her cult spread rapidly after the Second World War, especially in her native Poland. Her writings were initially placed under restrictions (1959–1978) over concerns about translation errors and theological misunderstandings; the restrictions were lifted by John Paul II, who had championed her cause as Archbishop of Kraków. He canonized her in 2000 and on the same day established Divine Mercy Sunday for the universal Church.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily; *Diary of Saint Faustina*
