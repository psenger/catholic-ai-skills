# 7. Second Council of Nicaea (787)

### 7. Second Council of Nicaea (787)

**Convoked by:** Pope Adrian I (and the Empress Irene)
**Heresy addressed:** Iconoclasm — the destruction of sacred images, fostered by a series of Byzantine emperors and supported by some bishops
**Key teachings:**
- Restored the veneration of images
- Distinguished *latria* (the adoration owed to God alone) from *douleia* (the relative honor given to saints) and *hyperdoulia* (the higher honor given to the Mother of God)
- Defined that the honor paid to an image passes to the prototype represented by it

Nicaea II is the council of the Incarnation as ground for sacred art. The Iconoclasts argued from the Decalogue that all sacred images were idolatrous. The council answered that the Incarnation changes the situation: God who could not be depicted under the Old Covenant has made himself depictable by becoming flesh. The Catechism cites Nicaea II at length in its treatment of the first commandment (CCC 2129–2132). This is the last council fully shared between Rome and the East — and the last one the Eastern Orthodox count among the ecumenical councils.
