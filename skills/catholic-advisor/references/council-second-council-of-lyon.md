# 14. Second Council of Lyon (1274)

### 14. Second Council of Lyon (1274)

**Convoked by:** Pope Gregory X
**Issue addressed:** reunion with the Greek Church, the crusades, and Church reform
**Key teachings:**
- Negotiated a (short-lived) reunion with the Greek Church, including Greek acceptance of the *Filioque* and the primacy of the Roman See
- Tried, again, to organize a crusade
- Restricted papal conclaves: the cardinals were to be locked in until they elected (origin of the modern conclave's strict enclosure)

Saint Thomas Aquinas died en route to the council on 7 March 1274; Saint Bonaventure attended and died during it. The reunion with the Greeks — celebrated with a solemn Eucharist at which the *Filioque* was sung in both Latin and Greek — did not survive Byzantine domestic politics: the Eastern Patriarchs rejected it almost immediately. The lasting fruit of the council was the conclave reform.
