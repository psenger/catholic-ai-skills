# The Didache (late 1st century)

### The Didache (late 1st century)

**Era:** Apostolic | **Feast day:** N/A (a document, not a person)
**Major work:** *The Didache* — *The Teaching of the Twelve Apostles*

The Didache is a short manual of Christian moral teaching, liturgy, and Church order, composed (in the form we have it) in the late first or early second century — earlier, in much of its material, than some New Testament writings. It opens with the "Two Ways" — the way of life and the way of death — a catechetical scheme common in Jewish moral instruction. It contains the earliest Eucharistic prayers outside the New Testament, the earliest explicit reference to baptism by triple immersion or affusion in the name of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit (Didache 7), and disciplinary canons concerning itinerant prophets and the appointment of bishops and deacons. The document was lost for centuries and rediscovered in a Greek manuscript at Constantinople in 1873. It is the earliest church order we possess.
