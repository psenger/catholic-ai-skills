# Saint Stephen the Protomartyr (d. c. 34)

### Saint Stephen the Protomartyr (d. c. 34)

**Feast day:** December 26 | **Age at death:** unknown (young man)
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** the first Christian martyr; one of the seven original deacons (Acts 6:5); stoned at Jerusalem with the consent of the future Saint Paul

The fullest biblical account of any martyr's death is the account of Stephen's in Acts 6–7. Stephen was one of seven men chosen to serve at table so that the apostles could devote themselves to prayer and the ministry of the word. He preached with such power that the council of Jerusalem confronted him; he answered with a long survey of salvation history culminating in the charge that they had killed the Just One. They stoned him; he died praying, "Lord, do not hold this sin against them" (Acts 7:60). His feast is the day after Christmas — first witness to the Word made flesh.
