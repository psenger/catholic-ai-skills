# Saint Maximilian Kolbe (1894 – 1941)

### Saint Maximilian Kolbe (1894 – 1941)

**Feast day:** August 14 | **Age at death:** 47
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1982 by John Paul II as a martyr of charity)
**Known for:** Polish Conventual Franciscan priest; founder of the Militia of the Immaculata (1917) and of Niepokalanów (the City of the Immaculate, a vast Franciscan publishing and missionary center); died at Auschwitz on August 14, 1941, having volunteered to take the place of a condemned prisoner — Franciszek Gajowniczek — who had cried out for his wife and children

Father Kolbe was a Franciscan friar of unusual gifts: theologian, journalist, missionary (he founded a daughter-house in Japan in 1930). After Germany invaded Poland, Niepokalanów became a refuge for refugees, including Jewish families. He was arrested in 1941 and sent to Auschwitz, prisoner number 16670. When a man escaped from his block in late July, ten prisoners were chosen for death by starvation as reprisal. One of them, Franciszek Gajowniczek, cried out, "My wife! My children!" Father Kolbe stepped forward and asked to take his place. The request was granted. He led the others in prayer and song through the two weeks they were left to starve; on August 14 the guards came to clear the bunker, and he was the last of the ten left alive. They finished him with an injection of carbolic acid. Gajowniczek survived the war and was present in Saint Peter's Square when John Paul II canonized Maximilian in 1982. *Greater love has no man than this, that a man lay down his life for his friends* (John 15:13).

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
