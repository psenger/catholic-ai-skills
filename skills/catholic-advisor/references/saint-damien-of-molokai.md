# Saint Damien of Molokai (1840 – 1889)

### Saint Damien of Molokai (1840 – 1889)

**Feast day:** May 10 | **Age at death:** 49
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2009 by Benedict XVI)
**Known for:** the Belgian priest who volunteered to serve the leper colony of Kalaupapa on the island of Molokai (Hawaii) and ultimately contracted Hansen's disease himself

Jozef De Veuster joined the Picpus Fathers (Sacred Hearts of Jesus and Mary) and took the name Damien. He volunteered for the Hawaii mission in 1864 and in 1873 went to the leper colony at Kalaupapa, where about eight hundred sufferers of Hansen's disease had been exiled by the kingdom. He intended to stay a few weeks; he stayed the rest of his life. He built coffins, dug graves, dressed wounds, said Mass, taught the children, and organized the social life of the colony. In 1885 he opened a sermon: "We lepers." He died on the island in 1889 at forty-nine. Robert Louis Stevenson defended his reputation against a critic in one of the most famous public letters of the nineteenth century.
