# 3. Council of Ephesus (431)

### 3. Council of Ephesus (431)

**Convoked by:** Pope Celestine I (presided in his name by Cyril of Alexandria)
**Heresy addressed:** Nestorianism — the teaching of Nestorius, Patriarch of Constantinople, that Christ was two persons (one human, one divine) and that Mary could be called Mother of Christ but not Mother of God
**Key teachings:**
- Defined that Christ is one divine person in two natures
- Defined Mary as *Theotokos* — God-bearer, Mother of God — because she gave birth to Christ in his single person, and that person is God
- Condemned the teaching of Nestorius and deposed him

The council assembled at Ephesus, the city where, according to tradition, Mary spent her last years in the care of the apostle John. The dogmatic issue was christological but the popular symbol was Marian: when the council's decision in favor of *Theotokos* was announced, the people of Ephesus accompanied the bishops home with torches and chanted hymns. Cyril of Alexandria's twelve anathemas against Nestorius shaped the council's definitions. Ephesus did not invent the title *Theotokos*; it was already common in Christian devotion. What the council did was to insist that the title be retained, because to deny it was to imply that the one born of Mary was not God.
