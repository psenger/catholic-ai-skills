# Saint Peter (d. c. 64–67) — Pope until c. 64–67

### Saint Peter (d. c. 64–67) — Pope until c. 64–67

**Birth name:** Simon bar Jonah | **Feast day:** June 29 (with Paul) | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint
**Known for:** chief of the apostles; first Bishop of Rome; principal architect of the early Church's missionary expansion to the Gentiles

Peter was called by Jesus on the Sea of Galilee, given a new name (Matthew 16:18), confessed Christ as the Son of God at Caesarea Philippi, denied him three times in the high priest's courtyard, was restored by the risen Lord on the shore of Tiberias (John 21), and led the apostolic Church after Pentecost. He moved his ministry from Jerusalem to Antioch and finally to Rome, where he died as a martyr under Nero, traditionally crucified upside down (Origen, via Eusebius). The excavations under Saint Peter's Basilica conducted from 1939 to 1968 identified with high probability the bones of Peter in a grave directly beneath the high altar. The first Letter of Peter is addressed from "Babylon" — a coded reference to Rome (1 Peter 5:13). See `apostles.md` for the fuller treatment of Peter as apostle.
