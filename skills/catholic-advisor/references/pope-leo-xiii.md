# Leo XIII (1810 – 1903) — Pope 1878–1903

### Leo XIII (1810 – 1903) — Pope 1878–1903

**Birth name:** Vincenzo Gioacchino Pecci | **Age at death:** 93
**Canonization status:** not under cause
**Known for:** *Rerum Novarum* (1891) — the foundational document of modern Catholic social teaching; eighty-six encyclicals (more than any other pope); the revival of Thomism (*Aeterni Patris*, 1879)

*Rerum Novarum* was the Catholic Church's principal response to the social crises of the industrial age — the conditions of workers, the rise of socialism, the relation of capital and labor. It defended the rights of workers to a just wage, to safe conditions, to form labor unions, while also defending private property and rejecting Marxist socialism. The encyclical inaugurated the tradition that produced *Quadragesimo Anno* (1931), *Mater et Magistra* (1961), *Pacem in Terris* (1963), *Populorum Progressio* (1967), *Laborem Exercens* (1981), *Centesimus Annus* (1991), *Caritas in Veritate* (2009), *Laudato Si'* (2015), and *Fratelli Tutti* (2020). Leo XIII also opened the Vatican Archives to scholars (1881) — an act of intellectual courage that established the modern Catholic engagement with historical-critical scholarship.
