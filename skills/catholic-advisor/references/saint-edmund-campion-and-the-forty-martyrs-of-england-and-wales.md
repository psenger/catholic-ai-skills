# Saint Edmund Campion and the Forty Martyrs of England and Wales (16th–17th centuries)

### Saint Edmund Campion and the Forty Martyrs of England and Wales (16th–17th centuries)

**Feast day:** October 25 (the Forty Martyrs); December 1 (Campion individually) | **Ages at death:** 41 (Campion)
**Canonization status:** Saints (the Forty Martyrs canonized as a group 1970 by Paul VI)
**Known for:** Catholic priests (and lay men and women) executed during the Elizabethan and Jacobean persecutions of Catholicism in England, 1535–1681

The forty figures canonized by Paul VI in 1970 are a representative selection of the hundreds of Catholics martyred during the English Reformation and afterward. Edmund Campion, an Oxford scholar who became a Jesuit and returned secretly to England in 1580 to minister to recusants, was arrested in 1581 and hanged, drawn, and quartered at Tyburn at age forty-one. His *Decem Rationes* — the ten arguments he set out for the Catholic faith — was distributed at his hanging. The group canonized in 1970 also includes Margaret Clitherow (a butcher's wife pressed to death for harboring priests), Edmund Arrowsmith, Henry Walpole, and Philip Howard, Earl of Arundel, who died in the Tower after eleven years of imprisonment.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily (1970)
