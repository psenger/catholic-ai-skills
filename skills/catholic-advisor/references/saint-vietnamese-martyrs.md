# The Vietnamese Martyrs (Saint Andrew Dung-Lac and Companions, d. 1745–1862)

### The Vietnamese Martyrs (Saint Andrew Dung-Lac and Companions, d. 1745–1862)

**Feast day:** November 24 | **Number canonized as a group:** 117
**Canonization status:** Saints (canonized 1988 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** Vietnamese Catholics — priests, religious, and laity, Vietnamese and foreign — martyred during the persecutions in Vietnam from the eighteenth into the nineteenth centuries

The 117 Vietnamese Martyrs canonized in 1988 represent an estimated 130,000 Vietnamese Catholics killed in the persecutions under various dynasties. Andrew Dung-Lac, a Vietnamese diocesan priest, was beheaded in 1839. The persecutions were particularly intense under the emperors Minh Mang and Tu Duc; the 1851 imperial edict prescribing the death penalty for any baptized Catholic produced thousands of martyrdoms.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily (1988)
