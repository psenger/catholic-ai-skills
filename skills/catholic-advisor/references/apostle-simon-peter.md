# Saint Simon Peter (d. c. 64–67)

### Saint Simon Peter (d. c. 64–67)

**Feast day:** June 29 (with Paul) | **Age at death:** unknown (likely 60s–70s) | **Martyrdom:** crucified upside-down in Rome under Nero, c. 64–67 (traditional account; attested by Origen via Eusebius, *Ecclesiastical History* III.1)
**Symbol in iconography:** crossed keys (the keys of the kingdom, Matthew 16:19); inverted cross
**Known for:** first among the apostles, the rock on which Christ built the Church, first pope

Simon, son of Jonah, a fisherman from Bethsaida and Capernaum, was called by Jesus together with his brother Andrew (Mark 1:16–17). At Caesarea Philippi he confessed Jesus as the Messiah, the Son of the living God, and received in reply the new name Cephas/Peter ("Rock") and the keys of the kingdom: "You are Peter, and on this rock I will build my church, and the gates of Hades shall not prevail against it. I will give you the keys of the kingdom of heaven" (Matthew 16:16–19). He walked on water briefly and sank when his faith faltered (Matthew 14:28–31). He denied Christ three times in the courtyard of the high priest and wept bitterly (Luke 22:54–62). After the Resurrection, by the Sea of Tiberias, Jesus restored him with a threefold question and a threefold commission: "Feed my lambs… Tend my sheep… Feed my sheep" (John 21:15–17). He led the Jerusalem community after Pentecost, opened the gospel to the Gentiles through the household of Cornelius (Acts 10), and ultimately died in Rome under Nero. His tomb beneath the high altar of St. Peter's Basilica has been excavated and the bones identified as authentically first-century with high probability.
