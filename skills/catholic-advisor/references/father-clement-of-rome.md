# Saint Clement of Rome (d. c. 99)

### Saint Clement of Rome (d. c. 99)

**Era:** Apostolic | **Feast day:** November 23 | **Age at death:** unknown
**Doctor of the Church:** No (though revered as a Father and pope)
**Major works:** First Letter to the Corinthians (1 Clement, c. AD 96)
**Known for:** the earliest Christian writing outside the New Testament; the fourth pope; the first written witness to the principle of apostolic succession outside Scripture

Clement was the fourth Bishop of Rome (after Peter, Linus, and Anacletus). 1 Clement is the most important sub-apostolic document we possess: an extended letter from the Roman Church to the Corinthians, addressing a schism in which younger members had deposed the lawfully appointed presbyters. The letter intervenes from Rome in the affairs of a distant local church — a striking early datum on the Roman primacy. The letter teaches that the apostles, foreseeing controversy, appointed bishops and gave the further command that "when these should fall asleep, other approved men should succeed to their ministry" (1 Clement 44). This is the doctrine of apostolic succession in its earliest non-scriptural form. Tradition holds that Clement was exiled to the Crimea and martyred by being thrown into the sea tied to an anchor; the historical core of these accounts cannot be verified.
