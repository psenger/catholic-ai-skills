# Francis (1936 – 2025) — Pope 2013–2025

### Francis (1936 – 2025) — Pope 2013–2025

**Birth name:** Jorge Mario Bergoglio | **Birthplace:** Buenos Aires, Argentina | **Age at death:** 88
**Canonization status:** not yet under cause
**Reign:** March 13, 2013 – April 21, 2025 (twelve years, one month)
**Known for:** the first pope from the Americas; the first Jesuit pope; the first pope to take the name Francis (in honor of Francis of Assisi); the encyclicals *Lumen Fidei* (2013, co-authored with Benedict XVI), *Laudato Si'* (2015) on the care of our common home, *Fratelli Tutti* (2020) on fraternity and social friendship, *Dilexit Nos* (2024) on devotion to the Sacred Heart; the apostolic exhortations *Evangelii Gaudium* (2013), *Amoris Laetitia* (2016), *Gaudete et Exsultate* (2018), *Querida Amazonia* (2020); the Synod on Synodality (2021–2024); the revision of CCC 2267 declaring the death penalty inadmissible (2018); the historic 2019 Document on Human Fraternity signed with Grand Imam Ahmad al-Tayyeb at Abu Dhabi

Francis was the first pope from the Americas and the first Jesuit ever elected. His chosen name — Francis — pointed to the saint of Assisi: poverty, peace, and care for creation. His pontificate was marked by a strong pastoral accent: outreach to the periphery (geographic and existential), insistence on the Church as a "field hospital after battle" (his recurring image), and a distinctive engagement with the world's poor, with migrants, and with the environment. *Laudato Si'* is the first papal encyclical devoted to environmental theology and the integration of ecological with social and economic ethics. *Fratelli Tutti* extends Catholic social teaching to a global vision of fraternity across religious and national boundaries. Francis died at Domus Sanctae Marthae on Easter Monday, April 21, 2025, age 88. He was buried at the Basilica of Saint Mary Major.
