# Saint Frances Xavier Cabrini (1850 – 1917)

### Saint Frances Xavier Cabrini (1850 – 1917)

**Feast day:** November 13 | **Age at death:** 67
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1946 by Pius XII — the first American citizen canonized); declared Patroness of Immigrants by Pius XII in 1950
**Known for:** founder of the Missionary Sisters of the Sacred Heart of Jesus (1880); apostle to Italian immigrants in the United States

Frances Cabrini was born in Lombardy, the youngest of thirteen children. She had wanted to be a missionary to China; Pope Leo XIII redirected her — "not east, but west" — to the Italian immigrants of New York. She crossed the Atlantic twenty-three times in the next twenty-eight years, founding sixty-seven institutions across the Americas — schools, hospitals, orphanages — serving Italian immigrants in cities from New York to New Orleans to Buenos Aires. She became an American citizen in 1909 and died in Chicago in 1917.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
