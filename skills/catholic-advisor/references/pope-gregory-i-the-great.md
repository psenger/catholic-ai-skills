# Saint Gregory I "the Great" (c. 540 – 604) — Pope 590–604

### Saint Gregory I "the Great" (c. 540 – 604) — Pope 590–604

**Birth name:** Gregory | **Feast day:** September 3 | **Age at death:** approximately 64
**Canonization status:** Saint; Doctor of the Church (proclaimed 1295, one of the four original Latin Doctors)
**Known for:** the *Pastoral Rule*; the *Moralia in Job*; the conversion of England (he sent Augustine of Canterbury); the reform of the liturgy and chant (Gregorian chant is named for him); the founding of the medieval papacy

Gregory was a Roman aristocrat who became a monk in his own family palace, then a papal apocrisiary at Constantinople, and finally — unwillingly — pope. He titled himself *Servus Servorum Dei*, "Servant of the Servants of God," a title every subsequent pope has retained. His missionary enterprise to convert the Anglo-Saxons of England — sending Augustine of Canterbury with forty monks in 596 — was decisive in the Christianization of northern Europe.
