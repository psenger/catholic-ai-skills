# Saint John of Damascus / John Damascene (c. 675 – c. 749)

### Saint John of Damascus / John Damascene (c. 675 – c. 749)

**Era:** Greek | **Feast day:** December 4 | **Age at death:** approximately 74
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1890 by Leo XIII)
**Major works:** *Fount of Knowledge* (in three parts: *Dialectica*, *On Heresies*, *On the Orthodox Faith*); *Three Treatises on the Divine Images*
**Known for:** the last of the Greek Fathers; the principal patristic defender of sacred images against Iconoclasm; a foundational synthesizer of Greek theology

John lived under Muslim rule in Damascus — a fact that ironically protected him from Byzantine imperial reprisal during the Iconoclast controversy. His three Treatises on the Divine Images, written in defense of the veneration of icons against the iconoclast emperors, became the standard theological resource of the second Council of Nicaea (787): the honor paid to an image, John argued, passes to the prototype represented by it. His *On the Orthodox Faith* — the third part of the *Fount of Knowledge* — is the most influential systematic theology of the patristic age, drawn on by both East and West for centuries. Thomas Aquinas knew it well. With John, the patristic period in the East formally closes.
