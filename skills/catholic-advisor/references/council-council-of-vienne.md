# 15. Council of Vienne (1311–1312)

### 15. Council of Vienne (1311–1312)

**Convoked by:** Pope Clement V (under pressure from King Philip IV of France)
**Issue addressed:** the suppression of the Knights Templar
**Key teachings:**
- Suppressed the Templars (though notably without finding them formally guilty of the charges leveled)
- Condemned certain Beghard and Beguine teachings
- Some disciplinary reforms

The suppression of the Templars under royal pressure was one of the more troubling acts of an Avignon papacy increasingly subordinated to French royal interests. The council disposed of the Templars' substantial property by transferring much of it to the Knights Hospitaller. It produced little of dogmatic interest.
