# Saint Polycarp of Smyrna (c. 69 – c. 155)

### Saint Polycarp of Smyrna (c. 69 – c. 155)

**Era:** Apostolic | **Feast day:** February 23 | **Age at death:** 86 (his own statement at his trial)
**Doctor of the Church:** No
**Major works:** Letter to the Philippians; his martyrdom is preserved in the *Martyrdom of Polycarp*, the earliest detailed account of a Christian martyrdom outside the New Testament
**Known for:** a personal disciple of John the Apostle; bishop of Smyrna; martyred by burning under the Emperor Antoninus Pius

Polycarp is one of the irreplaceable links between the apostolic generation and the patristic age. Irenaeus, who heard him preach in his youth, reports that Polycarp had been instructed by the apostles and had conversed with many who had seen the Lord. When arrested in old age and ordered to revile Christ, Polycarp answered: "Eighty and six years have I served him, and he never did me any wrong. How then can I blaspheme my King, who saved me?" (*Martyrdom* 9). He was bound to the stake and burned; the contemporary account (preserved in Eusebius and in independent manuscript) reports that the flames formed a vault around him and that he had to be finished off with a dagger. His Letter to the Philippians is largely a tissue of New Testament quotations — itself a precious witness to which New Testament writings were already accepted as authoritative by AD 110.
