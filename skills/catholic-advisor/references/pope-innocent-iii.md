# Innocent III (c. 1160 – 1216) — Pope 1198–1216

### Innocent III (c. 1160 – 1216) — Pope 1198–1216

**Birth name:** Lotario dei Conti di Segni | **Age at death:** approximately 56
**Canonization status:** not canonized
**Known for:** the high-water mark of medieval papal power; the Fourth Lateran Council (1215); approval of the Franciscans and Dominicans; the Fourth Crusade (which he condemned when it was diverted to sack Constantinople in 1204)

Innocent was elected at thirty-seven, the youngest pope of his age and one of the most consequential. He approved the rules of the Order of Friars Minor (Francis) and the Order of Preachers (Dominic), unleashing the mendicant movement that revitalized medieval Catholicism. He summoned the Fourth Lateran Council, the most important medieval council. He intervened in the affairs of every major European kingdom. The Fourth Crusade was a disaster: diverted by Venetian commercial interests, the crusaders sacked the Christian city of Constantinople in 1204 and broke the unity of Christendom in a wound that has never fully healed. Innocent denounced the act in furious terms but could not undo it.
