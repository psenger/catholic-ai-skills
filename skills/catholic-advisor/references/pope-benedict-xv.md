# Benedict XV (1854 – 1922) — Pope 1914–1922

### Benedict XV (1854 – 1922) — Pope 1914–1922

**Birth name:** Giacomo della Chiesa | **Age at death:** 67
**Canonization status:** not under cause
**Known for:** repeated peace appeals during the First World War (especially the Seven-Point Peace Plan of August 1917); the encyclical *Maximum Illud* (1919) on missions

Benedict XV was elected as the First World War was beginning. He maintained strict neutrality between the belligerents — a stance that made him popular with neither side. His repeated appeals for an end to the "useless slaughter" (his phrase) and his organization of relief for prisoners and refugees were the principal Catholic responses to the war. *Maximum Illud* (1919) called for the indigenization of the Catholic missions: training of local clergy, formation of native bishops, freedom from European colonial influence.
