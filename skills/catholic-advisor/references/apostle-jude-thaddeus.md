# Saint Jude Thaddeus (d. c. 65)

### Saint Jude Thaddeus (d. c. 65)

**Feast day:** October 28 (with Simon) | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** tradition has him martyred with Simon the Zealot in Persia
**Symbol in iconography:** a club; an image of Christ at his breast (because of the legend of Abgar)
**Known for:** patron of impossible causes; the Letter of Jude

Jude — also called Thaddeus and Lebbaeus in the apostolic lists — is named in all four lists of the Twelve (Matthew 10:3; Mark 3:18; Luke 6:16; Acts 1:13), with Luke and Acts using the name "Judas of James" (the brother or son of James) to distinguish him from Judas Iscariot. At the Last Supper he asks Jesus: "Lord, how is it that you will reveal yourself to us, and not to the world?" (John 14:22), prompting Jesus' teaching that the Father and the Son will come and make their home with those who love him. The brief Letter of Jude is attributed to him and contains a memorable warning against doctrinal corruption and a great closing doxology (Jude 24–25). Tradition pairs him with Simon the Zealot in a Persian mission and a joint martyrdom — hence their shared feast day. He became, by an obscure devotional history, the patron of impossible causes; his veneration in this role grew especially in the twentieth century and remains intense.
