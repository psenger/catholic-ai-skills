# Saint Óscar Romero (1917 – 1980)

### Saint Óscar Romero (1917 – 1980)

**Feast day:** March 24 | **Age at death:** 62
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2018 by Francis)
**Known for:** Archbishop of San Salvador; outspoken defender of the poor and of the victims of the Salvadoran military regime; assassinated while saying Mass on March 24, 1980

Romero was a quiet, conservative priest and bishop until his elevation to the Archdiocese of San Salvador in 1977. The murder of his friend, the Jesuit priest Rutilio Grande, by government-aligned forces three weeks after his installation transformed him. For the next three years he used his Sunday homilies — broadcast nationally — to document, by name, the disappeared, the tortured, the killed. He was warned repeatedly and refused to be silent. On March 23, 1980, in his Sunday homily, he commanded the soldiers of El Salvador in the name of God to stop the killing. The next day, while elevating the chalice at Mass in the chapel of the Hospital of Divine Providence, he was shot through the heart by a sniper. He died at the altar. Pope Francis canonized him in 2018 alongside Paul VI.
