# Saint Alphonsus Liguori (1696 – 1787)

### Saint Alphonsus Liguori (1696 – 1787)

**Feast day:** August 1 | **Age at death:** 90
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1871 by Pius IX)
**Known for:** founder of the Redemptorists (Congregation of the Most Holy Redeemer, 1732); the foremost moral theologian of the modern Catholic tradition; *Moral Theology* (the *Theologia Moralis*, 9 vols., 1748–1785); *The Glories of Mary*; the patron of moral theologians and confessors

Alphonsus was a brilliant Neapolitan lawyer who abandoned his career after winning, through carelessness, a case he should have lost. Ordained a priest, he devoted himself first to the abandoned poor of Naples and then to mission among the peasants of southern Italy. His Redemptorist community became one of the major missionary congregations of the modern Church. His *Moral Theology* — moderate, pastoral, and exact — became the standard manual for Catholic confessors. His Marian devotional writing, especially *The Glories of Mary*, shaped popular piety for two centuries.
