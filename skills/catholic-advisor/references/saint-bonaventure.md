# Saint Bonaventure (1221 – 1274)

### Saint Bonaventure (1221 – 1274)

**Feast day:** July 15 | **Age at death:** 53
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1588 by Sixtus V); *Doctor Seraphicus*
**Known for:** Minister General of the Franciscan Order (1257–1274); the *Journey of the Mind into God* (*Itinerarium Mentis in Deum*, 1259); friend and contemporary of Aquinas; cardinal-bishop of Albano; died at the Council of Lyon II while a participant

Bonaventure (born John of Fidanza) joined the Franciscans, studied with the great Franciscan masters at Paris, and was elected Minister General of the Order at thirty-five. He was the principal architect of the early Franciscan intellectual tradition — affective, mystical, and Augustinian in temper, as a counterpoint to the more rationalist Dominican school of Thomas. His *Itinerarium* traces the soul's ascent to God in six stages, modeled on the six wings of the Seraph that Francis saw at La Verna. He died at the Council of Lyon II in 1274 — the same year as Thomas Aquinas.
