# Saint Paul VI (1897 – 1978) — Pope 1963–1978

### Saint Paul VI (1897 – 1978) — Pope 1963–1978

**Birth name:** Giovanni Battista Montini | **Feast day:** May 29 | **Age at death:** 80
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized October 14, 2018, by Pope Francis)
**Known for:** completing the Second Vatican Council; promulgating the reformed liturgy (the *Novus Ordo Missae*, 1969); the encyclical *Humanae Vitae* (1968) reaffirming Catholic teaching against artificial contraception; *Populorum Progressio* (1967) on the development of peoples; *Evangelii Nuntiandi* (1975) on evangelization

Paul VI presided over the most thorough institutional reform of the Catholic Church since the Council of Trent: the reform of the liturgy, the reform of the Roman Curia, the institution of the worldwide Synod of Bishops, the simplification of the cardinalate, the establishment of new dicasteries. His most contested act was *Humanae Vitae*, which reaffirmed the Catholic teaching that every conjugal act must remain open to the transmission of life — a teaching that has been the subject of intense debate ever since but that the subsequent Magisterium (John Paul II, Benedict XVI, Francis) has reaffirmed. Paul VI was the first pope to travel widely outside Italy: to the Holy Land in 1964 (where he met Patriarch Athenagoras of Constantinople in the first such meeting since 1438), to India, to the United Nations, to Africa, to South America.
