# Saint Francis of Assisi (1181/1182 – 1226)

### Saint Francis of Assisi (1181/1182 – 1226)

**Feast day:** October 4 | **Age at death:** approximately 44
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1228 by Gregory IX, two years after his death); declared co-patron of Italy (with Catherine of Siena) by Pius XII in 1939
**Known for:** founder of the Order of Friars Minor (Franciscans); the Canticle of the Creatures (the *Canticle of Brother Sun*); the stigmata (received at La Verna in 1224 — the first stigmatic in Christian history); the Christmas crèche (he created the first living nativity scene at Greccio in 1223)

The son of a wealthy cloth merchant, Francis at twenty-four stripped himself of his clothes in the public square of Assisi and renounced his inheritance before the bishop. He took as his bride "Lady Poverty" and began the radical life of evangelical poverty that would attract thousands of followers within a decade. His Order — the Friars Minor, the Lesser Brothers — and his sister Clare's Poor Ladies (the Poor Clares) reshaped medieval European Christianity. His Canticle, composed near the end of his life when he was nearly blind and in great pain, addresses Brother Sun and Sister Moon, Brother Fire and Sister Water, Sister Death; it is the foundational lyric of Christian ecological spirituality (a connection Pope Francis made explicit in *Laudato Si'*). The stigmata he received at La Verna in 1224 — wounds matching the wounds of Christ — were verified after his death by many witnesses. He died on October 3, 1226, lying on the bare earth at his request, singing the Canticle.
