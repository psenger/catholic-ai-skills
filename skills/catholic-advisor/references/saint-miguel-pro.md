# Blessed Miguel Pro (1891 – 1927)

### Blessed Miguel Pro (1891 – 1927)

**Feast day:** November 23 | **Age at death:** 36
**Canonization status:** Blessed (beatified 1988 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** Mexican Jesuit priest; clandestine pastor during the Cristero persecution; executed by firing squad, arms outstretched in cruciform, crying *Viva Cristo Rey*

Miguel Agustín Pro Juárez was a Jesuit priest who returned to Mexico in 1926 just as the Calles government's anticlerical measures were taking full effect. For over a year he served as a clandestine priest, often in disguise, hearing confessions in cafés and saying Mass in private houses. He was arrested in November 1927 on the basis of false evidence linking him to an assassination attempt on the former president. Without trial he was shot by firing squad on November 23, 1927. The execution was photographed at the order of President Calles, who hoped the images would intimidate Catholic resistance; they produced the opposite effect. The photographs — Pro with arms outstretched, refusing the blindfold, crying *Viva Cristo Rey* as the rifles were raised — circulated through the country and have become some of the most reproduced Catholic images of the twentieth century.
