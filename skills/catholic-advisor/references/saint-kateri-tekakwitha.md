# Saint Kateri Tekakwitha (1656 – 1680)

### Saint Kateri Tekakwitha (1656 – 1680)

**Feast day:** July 14 | **Age at death:** 24
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2012 by Benedict XVI — the first Native American saint of North America)
**Known for:** "Lily of the Mohawks"; Algonquin-Mohawk Catholic; daughter of a Mohawk chief and a Christian Algonquin mother; baptized at age twenty; lived the last years of her life at the Jesuit mission at Kahnawake in present-day Quebec

Kateri's mother and father died of smallpox when she was four; the same outbreak scarred her face and weakened her sight. She was raised by her uncle, a Mohawk chief opposed to Christianity. She encountered Jesuit missionaries as a child, was instructed secretly, and was baptized at twenty by the Jesuit Jacques de Lamberville. Her conversion brought severe persecution from her community; she fled some two hundred miles to the Jesuit mission at Kahnawake near Montreal. She lived there for four years in great austerity and died at twenty-four. Witnesses reported that the smallpox scars vanished from her face within minutes of her death.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
