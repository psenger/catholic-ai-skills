# Saint Agnes of Rome (c. 291 – c. 304)

### Saint Agnes of Rome (c. 291 – c. 304)

**Feast day:** January 21 | **Age at death:** approximately 12–13
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** martyred under Diocletian for refusing marriage to the son of a Roman prefect, having consecrated her virginity to Christ; patroness of young women, of virgins, of betrothed couples

The earliest account is by Saint Ambrose, who wrote that Agnes was a girl of twelve or thirteen when she was led to martyrdom. The historical kernel — a young girl of Rome, martyred in the Diocletianic persecution — is solid; the developed details of the account are partly legendary. Her name is in the Roman Canon. The Latin pun on her name (Agnes/agnus, lamb) gave rise to the tradition that two lambs are blessed each year on her feast at her basilica at Sant'Agnese Fuori le Mura; the wool is woven into the pallia worn by metropolitan archbishops.
