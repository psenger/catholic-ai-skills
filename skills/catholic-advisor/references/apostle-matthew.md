# Saint Matthew (d. c. 1st century)

### Saint Matthew (d. c. 1st century)

**Feast day:** September 21 | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** tradition variously gives Ethiopia, Persia, or Syria; manner uncertain (sword, fire, or natural death are all attested in differing traditions)
**Symbol in iconography:** a winged man (the first living creature of Revelation 4:7, traditional symbol of the First Gospel); a money bag (his tax-collector past); a sword
**Known for:** First Gospel; the tax collector called from his customs post

Matthew (also called Levi, son of Alphaeus) was a tax collector — a *publican*, despised in Jewish society as both a collaborator with the Roman occupier and a man who lived by extortion. Jesus saw him "sitting at the tax booth, and he said to him, 'Follow me.' And he got up and followed him" (Matthew 9:9; cf. Mark 2:14, Luke 5:27). Matthew then made him a great banquet, attended by many tax collectors and sinners — which scandalized the Pharisees and prompted one of Jesus' most piercing answers: "Those who are well have no need of a physician, but those who are sick; go and learn what this means, 'I desire mercy, not sacrifice.' For I have come to call not the righteous but sinners" (Matthew 9:10–13). The First Gospel is attributed to him on the authority of Papias (c. AD 120). Tradition places his mission and martyrdom variously in Ethiopia or Persia. He is the patron of bankers, accountants, and tax collectors.
