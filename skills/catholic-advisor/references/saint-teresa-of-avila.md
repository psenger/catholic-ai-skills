# Saint Teresa of Ávila (1515 – 1582)

### Saint Teresa of Ávila (1515 – 1582)

**Feast day:** October 15 | **Age at death:** 67
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1970 by Paul VI — one of the first two women named Doctors)
**Known for:** the reformer of the Carmelite Order (the Discalced Carmelites); *The Interior Castle*; *The Way of Perfection*; her *Autobiography* (the *Vida*); the mystical "transverberation" (a vision in which a seraph pierced her heart with a fiery dart)

Teresa entered the Carmelite convent of the Incarnation at Ávila in 1535 and lived there for some twenty years before undergoing a profound spiritual conversion in midlife. Recognizing the relaxation that had crept into Spanish Carmelite life, she founded a new monastery at Ávila (Saint Joseph's, 1562) and devoted the rest of her life to founding similar reformed (Discalced) houses across Spain. Her mystical writings — especially *The Interior Castle*, which describes the seven "mansions" of the soul's progress toward union with God — are foundational for Catholic mystical theology.
