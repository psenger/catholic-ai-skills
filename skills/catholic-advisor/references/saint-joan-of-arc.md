# Saint Joan of Arc (1412 – 1431)

### Saint Joan of Arc (1412 – 1431)

**Feast day:** May 30 | **Age at death:** 19
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1920 by Benedict XV); declared a secondary patroness of France in 1922; co-patroness of France with Thérèse of Lisieux
**Known for:** the peasant girl of Domrémy who, at seventeen, led the armies of Charles VII to victory at Orléans (1429); burned at the stake at Rouen as a heretic by an ecclesiastical court controlled by the English (1431); rehabilitated by a papal court in 1456; canonized in 1920

Joan, the daughter of a peasant farmer of Lorraine, began at thirteen to hear voices she identified as Michael the Archangel, Catherine of Alexandria, and Margaret of Antioch. They commanded her to lead the king's armies to the relief of Orléans and to see Charles VII crowned at Reims. She did both, against every probability. Captured by the Burgundians and sold to the English, she was tried by a court of pro-English clerics led by Pierre Cauchon and convicted of heresy. She was burned at Rouen on May 30, 1431. A retrial under papal authority twenty-five years later cleared her name. She was canonized in 1920. Her story has shaped French national identity ever since.
