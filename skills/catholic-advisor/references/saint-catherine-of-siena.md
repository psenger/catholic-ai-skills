# Saint Catherine of Siena (1347 – 1380)

### Saint Catherine of Siena (1347 – 1380)

**Feast day:** April 29 | **Age at death:** 33
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1970 by Paul VI — one of the first two women named Doctors)
**Known for:** the lay Dominican who confronted popes and kings; persuaded Pope Gregory XI to return the papacy from Avignon to Rome (1377); the *Dialogue* (her great mystical-theological work, dictated in ecstasy); the stigmata

Catherine was the twenty-third (some say twenty-fifth) of twenty-five children of a Siena dyer. She refused marriage, became a Dominican tertiary at sixteen, and from her cell exercised an extraordinary moral influence. Her letters — to popes, to cardinals, to kings, to crusaders — are bracing and direct. Her *Dialogue* is one of the great mystical works of the Catholic tradition. She is co-patroness of Europe (with Edith Stein and Bridget of Sweden) and a patroness of Italy (with Francis of Assisi).
