# Saint Vincent de Paul (1581 – 1660)

### Saint Vincent de Paul (1581 – 1660)

**Feast day:** September 27 | **Age at death:** 79
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1737)
**Known for:** founder of the Congregation of the Mission (Vincentians, 1625) and co-founder (with Saint Louise de Marillac) of the Daughters of Charity (1633); the foremost organizer of Catholic charity in the modern era; the Saint Vincent de Paul Society (founded by Frédéric Ozanam in 1833 and named for him)

Vincent was a French diocesan priest captured by Barbary pirates as a young man, sold into slavery in Tunis, and escaped after two years. He returned to France and devoted the rest of his life to two great enterprises: the systematic evangelization and pastoral care of the rural poor (the Mission) and the corporal works of mercy on a society-wide scale (the Daughters of Charity, the confraternities, the foundling hospitals, the galley slaves whose conditions he labored to ameliorate). His was the largest single Catholic charitable enterprise of the seventeenth century.
