# 17. Council of Basel–Ferrara–Florence (1431–1445)

### 17. Council of Basel–Ferrara–Florence (1431–1445)

**Convoked by:** Pope Martin V; presided largely under Pope Eugene IV
**Issue addressed:** the relationship between pope and council after Constance, and a renewed attempt at reunion with the Greek Church
**Key teachings:**
- Negotiated, at Florence, the *Laetentur Caeli* decree of reunion with the Greek Church (1439) — acceptance of papal primacy, the *Filioque*, purgatory, and the use of unleavened or leavened bread according to local custom
- Negotiated separate decrees of reunion with the Armenians (*Exsultate Deo*, 1439) and the Copts (*Cantate Domino*, 1442)
- Reaffirmed the pope's authority against the Basel conciliarists
- Defined the seven sacraments

The Florentine reunion fell apart after the fall of Constantinople (1453), as the Byzantine population repudiated the union and the Eastern Patriarchates withdrew. The dogmatic and sacramental decrees of Florence, however, remained in force in the Catholic Church and significantly shaped the work of Trent.
