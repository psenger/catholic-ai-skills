# Saint Basil the Great (c. 330 – 379)

### Saint Basil the Great (c. 330 – 379)

**Era:** Greek (Cappadocian) | **Feast day:** January 2 (with Gregory of Nazianzus); in the East, January 1 | **Age at death:** approximately 49
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1568)
**Major works:** *On the Holy Spirit*; the *Long Rules* and *Short Rules* (his monastic rules); *Hexaemeron* (homilies on the six days of creation); numerous letters
**Known for:** one of the Three Holy Hierarchs of the Byzantine East (with Gregory of Nazianzus and John Chrysostom); the father of Eastern monasticism; the principal architect of the pneumatology of the First Council of Constantinople

Basil was born into a Christian family of extraordinary holiness: his grandmother Macrina the Elder, his parents Basil the Elder and Emmelia, his sister Macrina the Younger, and his brothers Gregory of Nyssa and Peter of Sebaste are all venerated as saints. He studied in Athens with his lifelong friend Gregory of Nazianzus and the future emperor Julian the Apostate. Drawn first to monasticism, he traveled to Egypt and Syria to study the eremitic and cenobitic traditions. His monastic rules — moderate, communal, and tied to a life of prayer and service to the poor — became the template for all subsequent Eastern monasticism. As bishop of Caesarea he founded a complex of hospitals, hospices, and homes for the poor (the *Basileias*) that anticipated the modern Catholic hospital system. His *On the Holy Spirit* shaped the pneumatology of Constantinople I, which Basil did not live to attend.
