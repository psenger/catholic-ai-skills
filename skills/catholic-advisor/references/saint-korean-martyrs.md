# The Korean Martyrs (Saint Andrew Kim Taegon, Saint Paul Chong Hasang, and Companions, d. 1839–1867)

### The Korean Martyrs (Saint Andrew Kim Taegon, Saint Paul Chong Hasang, and Companions, d. 1839–1867)

**Feast day:** September 20 | **Number canonized as a group:** 103
**Canonization status:** Saints (canonized 1984 by John Paul II — the first canonization ceremony held outside Rome since the Middle Ages, performed in Seoul)
**Known for:** Korean Catholics — laymen, women, and the first Korean-born priest, Andrew Kim Taegon — martyred during four major persecutions in nineteenth-century Korea

The Catholic Church in Korea began in the late eighteenth century in a way unique in the world: Korean scholars, hearing of Catholicism through books smuggled from China, formed a Catholic community before any foreign missionary set foot in Korea. When persecution came (in 1801, 1839, 1846, and 1866), thousands of Korean Catholics died. Andrew Kim Taegon was the first Korean-born priest, ordained in Shanghai in 1845 and martyred a year later at twenty-five. Paul Chong Hasang was a lay catechist. The 103 canonized in 1984 represent some 10,000 known Korean martyrs of the period.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily (1984)
