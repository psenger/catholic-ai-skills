# Saint John Baptist de La Salle (1651 – 1719)

### Saint John Baptist de La Salle (1651 – 1719)

**Feast day:** April 7 | **Age at death:** 67
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1900); declared Patron of Christian Teachers by Pius XII in 1950
**Known for:** founder of the Brothers of the Christian Schools (the Lasallian Brothers, 1680); the founder of modern Catholic educational practice for the poor

John Baptist de La Salle was a wealthy young French priest from Reims who, against the strenuous objections of his family, sold his inheritance, distributed it to the poor during the famine of 1683, and devoted his life to free education for boys of the working classes. His pedagogical innovations — classroom instruction (rather than individual tutoring), instruction in the vernacular (rather than Latin), the careful training of teachers, the systematic curriculum — shaped Catholic schooling for centuries. The Lasallian Brothers grew to become one of the major teaching congregations of the Catholic Church.
