# Saint Gregory of Nyssa (c. 335 – c. 394)

### Saint Gregory of Nyssa (c. 335 – c. 394)

**Era:** Greek (Cappadocian) | **Feast day:** January 10 | **Age at death:** approximately 59
**Doctor of the Church:** No (despite very strong patrological standing; the Catholic Church has not formally proclaimed him a Doctor, though he is universally regarded as one of the greatest mystical theologians of the Church)
**Major works:** *The Life of Moses*; *On the Soul and the Resurrection*; *The Great Catechism*; *On Virginity*
**Known for:** the speculative theologian among the Cappadocians; the architect of Christian mystical theology

Younger brother of Basil, Gregory of Nyssa took the speculative path. His *Life of Moses* reads the Exodus as a paradigm of the soul's journey to God — beginning in the light of natural reason, passing through the cloud of faith, ending in the divine darkness of *epektasis*, the unending progress of the soul into the inexhaustible mystery of God. This conception of the spiritual life as endless growth rather than static arrival is one of his great gifts to the Christian tradition. His treatise *On the Soul and the Resurrection*, written after the death of his sister Macrina, contains his finest writing on the human person, the body, the soul, and the resurrection of the dead.
