# Saint Damasus I (c. 305 – 384) — Pope 366–384

### Saint Damasus I (c. 305 – 384) — Pope 366–384

**Birth name:** Damasus | **Feast day:** December 11 | **Age at death:** approximately 79
**Canonization status:** Saint
**Known for:** commissioned Jerome to produce the Vulgate; restored the catacomb shrines of the Roman martyrs; first pope to call the Roman Church the "Apostolic See" in an authoritative way

Damasus appointed Jerome his secretary and entrusted him with the task of producing a definitive Latin Bible. He composed Latin epigrams for many of the catacomb tombs of martyrs — a number of which survive on stone in Roman basilicas. The Council of Constantinople (381) met during his pontificate and added the articles on the Holy Spirit to the Nicene Creed.
