# Saint Robert Bellarmine (1542 – 1621)

### Saint Robert Bellarmine (1542 – 1621)

**Feast day:** September 17 | **Age at death:** 78
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1931 by Pius XI)
**Known for:** the great Jesuit theologian of the Counter-Reformation; cardinal; consultor in the Galileo affair (1616); his *Disputationes* (a vast systematic refutation of Reformation theology)

Bellarmine entered the Jesuits, studied at Louvain, and became the leading Catholic systematic theologian responding to the Reformation. His *Disputationes de Controversiis* (1586–1593) was the Catholic counterpart to the great Reformed theologians of the age. He was made cardinal in 1599 and participated in the first Vatican investigation of Galileo (1616). His role there was conciliatory: he urged Galileo to present heliocentrism as hypothesis rather than as proven fact, given the scientific evidence then available — a counsel that, had Galileo accepted it, might have averted the later crisis. His personal life was austere and his pastoral writings on the soul's prayer and on Christian dying remain widely read.
