# Saint Gelasius I (d. 496) — Pope 492–496

### Saint Gelasius I (d. 496) — Pope 492–496

**Birth name:** Gelasius | **Feast day:** November 21 (locally) | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint
**Known for:** the "Two Swords" or "Two Powers" doctrine

In a letter to the Emperor Anastasius (494), Gelasius set out the classical doctrine of the relationship between sacred and secular authority: "There are two powers, august Emperor, by which this world is principally ruled: the consecrated authority of the priesthood and the royal power. Of these the responsibility of the priests is the greater, for they will have to render account at the divine judgment even for the kings of men" (Letter 12). The text became the standard medieval reference point for Church–state relations.
