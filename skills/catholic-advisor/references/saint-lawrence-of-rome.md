# Saint Lawrence of Rome (d. 258)

### Saint Lawrence of Rome (d. 258)

**Feast day:** August 10 | **Age at death:** young (probably 20s or 30s)
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** Roman deacon under Pope Sixtus II; martyred four days after the pope by being roasted on a gridiron (traditional account; modern scholarship considers beheading more likely, but the gridiron tradition is ancient and well-attested in iconography); patron of cooks and the poor

When Pope Sixtus II was led away to martyrdom, Lawrence is said to have run after him weeping, "Father, where are you going without your deacon?" Sixtus answered: "I am not leaving you, my son; in three days you will follow me." Lawrence used those three days to distribute the treasury of the Roman Church to the poor. When the prefect demanded the treasure, Lawrence presented the poor, the lame, and the sick of the city and said: "Here is the treasure of the Church." He was roasted on a gridiron — and (says the ancient tradition) joked from the flame: *Assum est, versa et manduca* — "I am cooked on this side; turn me over and eat." His name is in the Canon of the Mass. The historical core (his martyrdom under Valerian in 258) is solidly attested; the gridiron is part of the early popular tradition.
