# 10. Second Council of the Lateran (1139)

### 10. Second Council of the Lateran (1139)

**Convoked by:** Pope Innocent II
**Issue addressed:** the schism of Anacletus II (an antipope) and the disciplinary reform of the clergy
**Key teachings:**
- Confirmed the invalidity of marriages contracted by clerics in major orders
- Disciplinary canons against simony, usury, attacks on clergy

Lateran II resolved the schism that had divided the Church for eight years after the contested election of 1130. It also represents an important stage in the consolidation of clerical celibacy in the Latin Church: it did not merely forbid clerical marriage (already forbidden for centuries) but declared such marriages invalid — that is, no marriage at all in the eyes of canon law.
