# Saint Elizabeth Ann Seton (1774 – 1821)

### Saint Elizabeth Ann Seton (1774 – 1821)

**Feast day:** January 4 | **Age at death:** 46
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1975 by Paul VI — the first American-born saint)
**Known for:** convert from Episcopalianism (1805) following her husband's death; founder of the Sisters of Charity of Saint Joseph (1809, the first American religious community of women); pioneer of Catholic parochial schools in the United States

Elizabeth Bayley was born in New York City to a Protestant Episcopalian family and married William Seton at nineteen. When her husband died of tuberculosis in Italy in 1803, she was hosted by Catholic friends and was drawn into the Catholic Church; she was received in 1805 in New York. She moved with her five children to Baltimore and then to Emmitsburg, Maryland, where she founded the first community of Sisters of Charity in the United States (1809). The community ran the first Catholic free school for girls in the country — the seed of the American Catholic parochial school system. Paul VI canonized her in 1975.
