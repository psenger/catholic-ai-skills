# Saint Pius V (1504 – 1572) — Pope 1566–1572

### Saint Pius V (1504 – 1572) — Pope 1566–1572

**Birth name:** Antonio Ghislieri | **Feast day:** April 30 | **Age at death:** approximately 68
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1712)
**Known for:** the Tridentine Missal (the Roman Missal of 1570); the catechism of the Council of Trent (the Roman Catechism, 1566); the Holy League victory over the Ottoman fleet at Lepanto (October 7, 1571)

Pius V was a Dominican of austere personal life. He implemented the Tridentine reform with rigor: he promulgated the revised Roman Missal in 1570 (the Mass thereafter widely called the Tridentine Mass), the revised Roman Breviary (1568), and the Roman Catechism (1566). At his urging the Holy League — Spain, Venice, the Papal States, and others — assembled the fleet that crushed the Ottoman navy at Lepanto in October 1571, halting the Ottoman advance into the western Mediterranean. Pius attributed the victory to the rosaries prayed throughout Christendom on the day of the battle; the feast of Our Lady of the Rosary on October 7 commemorates this.
