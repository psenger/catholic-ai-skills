# Saint Thomas Aquinas (1225 – 1274)

### Saint Thomas Aquinas (1225 – 1274)

**Feast day:** January 28 | **Age at death:** 49
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1567 by Pius V); *Doctor Angelicus* ("the Angelic Doctor"); *Doctor Communis* ("the Common Doctor")
**Known for:** the *Summa Theologiae*; the *Summa Contra Gentiles*; the integration of Aristotelian philosophy with Christian theology; the Eucharistic hymns (*Pange Lingua*, *Adoro Te Devote*, *Tantum Ergo*, *Lauda Sion*); the standard reference theologian of Catholic theology

Thomas was born to a noble family near Aquino in Italy. Educated by Benedictines at Monte Cassino and at the new University of Naples, he joined the Dominicans against his family's wishes — they imprisoned him for nearly a year to try to dissuade him. Released, he studied under Albert the Great at Paris and at Cologne, taught at Paris and at the papal court, and produced the most influential corpus of theology in Western history. The *Summa Theologiae* — incomplete at his death — addresses every major question of theology in a method (objections, *sed contra*, response, replies to objections) that is the model of philosophical theology. On December 6, 1273, near the end of his life, after a profound mystical experience at Mass, he set aside his pen and said to his secretary Reginald: "All that I have written seems to me like straw compared to what has now been revealed to me." He died on the road to the Council of Lyon a few months later. His teaching was elevated to the rank of normative Catholic theology by Leo XIII's *Aeterni Patris* (1879) and remains the principal reference for Catholic systematic theology.
