# Saint Benedict of Nursia (c. 480 – c. 547)

### Saint Benedict of Nursia (c. 480 – c. 547)

**Feast day:** July 11 | **Age at death:** approximately 67
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation); declared Patron of Europe by Paul VI in 1964
**Known for:** the founder of Western monasticism; the *Rule of Saint Benedict* (c. 540); the monastery of Monte Cassino

Benedict was born to a noble family at Nursia and educated at Rome, but withdrew first to the wilderness and then, with disciples gathering around him, founded a series of monasteries in central Italy — the last and greatest at Monte Cassino. His *Rule*, written for that community, became the constitution of Western monasticism. It is a balanced, prudent document — neither the extreme asceticism of the desert nor the laxity that had crept into earlier Latin monasticism. *Ora et labora* — "pray and work" — summarizes its program. The Benedictine houses were the principal preservers of Western Christian culture through the Dark Ages and the soil from which Christian Europe grew.
