# The Uganda Martyrs (Saint Charles Lwanga and Companions, d. 1885–1887)

### The Uganda Martyrs (Saint Charles Lwanga and Companions, d. 1885–1887)

**Feast day:** June 3 | **Ages at death:** various (most in their teens and twenties)
**Canonization status:** Saints (canonized 1964 by Paul VI — the first sub-Saharan African saints)
**Known for:** twenty-two Catholic pages of the court of King Mwanga II of Buganda, burned alive at Namugongo on June 3, 1886, for refusing to participate in the king's homosexual abuse of them and for refusing to renounce their faith

King Mwanga II of Buganda (now central Uganda) sought to use his pages for sexual purposes. The Christian pages — both Catholic and Anglican — refused. The king's response was a comprehensive massacre. Charles Lwanga, the master of the pages, baptized those who had not yet received baptism on the eve of their executions. Twenty-two Catholics (with twenty-three Anglicans separately martyred) were burned alive at Namugongo. Paul VI canonized the twenty-two Catholics in 1964 during the Second Vatican Council; in his visit to Uganda in 1969 he prayed at the shrine of Namugongo and acknowledged the parallel witness of the Anglican martyrs.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily (1964)

## Source Note

The selection above covers approximately seventy major figures. The authoritative current source for Catholic feast days is the *Roman Martyrology* (current edition 2004, Holy See), supplemented by the *General Calendar of the Roman Rite*. Canonization documents for modern saints are available on vatican.va. The *Catholic Encyclopedia* (newadvent.org) is the standard reference for saints with ancient cults. Where this file notes that the historical core of a saint's life is uncertain, the cult is nevertheless ancient and the Church's veneration is in no way diminished.
