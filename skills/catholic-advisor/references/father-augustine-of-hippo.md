# Saint Augustine of Hippo (354 – 430)

### Saint Augustine of Hippo (354 – 430)

**Era:** Latin | **Feast day:** August 28 | **Age at death:** 75 (almost 76)
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1295 by Boniface VIII as one of the four original Latin Doctors; often simply called *the Doctor of Grace*)
**Major works:** *Confessions*; *City of God* (twenty-two books); *On the Trinity* (fifteen books); *On Christian Doctrine*; *On the Spirit and the Letter*; the anti-Pelagian writings (*On Nature and Grace*, *On Grace and Free Will*); commentaries on the Psalms, on John, on the Sermon on the Mount; vast correspondence
**Known for:** the giant of Western theology; bishop of Hippo Regius in North Africa for thirty-five years; the principal patristic theologian of grace, sin, the Church, the sacraments, and the philosophy of history

Augustine was born at Thagaste (modern Souk Ahras, Algeria), educated at Carthage, drifted into Manichaeism for nine years, abandoned it for Neoplatonism, and was at last drawn to Christianity through the preaching of Ambrose at Milan. His conversion in the garden, on hearing a child's voice chanting *tolle, lege* — "take up and read" — opening Paul's Letter to the Romans and reading 13:13–14 (Confessions VIII.12), is one of the most famous narratives in Christian literature. He was baptized by Ambrose on the Easter Vigil of 387, returned to North Africa, was ordained priest at Hippo in 391 and bishop in 395. The next thirty-five years produced a body of writing whose influence on Western Christianity — Catholic, Reformed, and other — exceeds that of any other Father. His *Confessions* is the first true autobiography in Western literature. His *City of God*, written as the Roman Empire was falling, is the founding text of Christian philosophy of history. His anti-Pelagian writings are the foundation of the Catholic doctrine of grace and original sin (received and developed at the Second Council of Orange in 529 and at Trent). He died in Hippo in 430 while the Vandals were besieging the city.
