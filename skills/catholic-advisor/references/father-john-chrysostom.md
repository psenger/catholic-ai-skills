# Saint John Chrysostom (c. 347 – 407)

### Saint John Chrysostom (c. 347 – 407)

**Era:** Greek (Antiochene) | **Feast day:** September 13 (formerly January 27) | **Age at death:** approximately 60
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1568); one of the Three Holy Hierarchs of the East
**Major works:** homilies on Genesis, Matthew, John, Acts, and the Pauline Letters; *On the Priesthood*; *On the Incomprehensibility of God*; the Divine Liturgy of Saint John Chrysostom (the principal Eucharistic liturgy of the Byzantine Rite)
**Known for:** *Chrysostom* — "Golden Mouth"; the supreme preacher of the patristic age; Patriarch of Constantinople; twice exiled, dying on the road

John was a monk and ascetic of Antioch before being raised to the priesthood in 386, when he began the preaching that would make him a legend. His homilies — on the Gospels, on Paul, on Genesis, on the saints — are among the most influential preaching texts in the Christian tradition. In 397 he was unwillingly chosen Patriarch of Constantinople and immediately set himself against the corruption of the court, the wealth of the empress Eudoxia, and the slack discipline of the city's clergy. He was exiled twice; the second time, his physical condition broken by the journey, he died in 407. His body was returned to Constantinople in 438 with great honor; in 1204 his relics were taken to Rome; in 2004 Pope John Paul II returned them to the Ecumenical Patriarchate.
