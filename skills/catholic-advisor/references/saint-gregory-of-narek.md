# Saint Gregory of Narek (c. 950 – c. 1003)

### Saint Gregory of Narek (c. 950 – c. 1003)

**Feast day:** February 27 | **Age at death:** approximately 53
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 2015 by Pope Francis — the first Armenian Doctor, and the first Doctor from the Armenian Apostolic Church)
**Known for:** Armenian monk, theologian, and mystical poet; the *Book of Lamentations* (also called the *Book of Prayer*, c. 1002)

Gregory of Narek was an Armenian monk and the greatest poet of the Armenian Christian tradition. His *Book of Lamentations* — ninety-five prayers in poetic dialogue with God — is among the most extraordinary mystical-poetic works in any Christian language. Pope Francis named him a Doctor of the Church in 2015 in a remarkable ecumenical gesture: Gregory belonged to the Armenian Apostolic Church, which is not in full communion with Rome. The proclamation honored a saint of the universal Christian tradition.

**Sources:** Vatican.va apostolic letter declaring him Doctor (2015)
