# Saint John Bosco (1815 – 1888)

### Saint John Bosco (1815 – 1888)

**Feast day:** January 31 | **Age at death:** 72
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1934 by Pius XI)
**Known for:** founder of the Salesians (Society of Saint Francis de Sales, 1859) and co-founder of the Salesian Sisters (with Maria Mazzarello); apostle of youth, especially poor boys; the "Preventive System" of education (kindness, reason, religion — instead of punishment)

Don Bosco grew up poor in the Piedmont of northern Italy. After ordination he devoted himself to the abandoned boys of industrializing Turin — the apprentices, the orphans, the immigrants. He founded the Oratory of Saint Francis de Sales as a place of catechesis, recreation, and trade-school training, and from it grew the Salesian Society. He was, by all accounts, an immensely warm and humorous man; his "Preventive System" of education — keeping the young so occupied with good things that they have no time for bad ones, won over by friendship rather than by force — became one of the most influential modern Catholic pedagogies. He died at seventy-two; his pupil Dominic Savio had predeceased him by decades.
