# 18. Fifth Council of the Lateran (1512–1517)

### 18. Fifth Council of the Lateran (1512–1517)

**Convoked by:** Pope Julius II; completed under Pope Leo X
**Issue addressed:** Church reform on the eve of the Reformation
**Key teachings:**
- Affirmed the immortality of the soul against Aristotelian materialist tendencies
- Reaffirmed papal authority against the conciliarists
- Various disciplinary reforms (preaching, the censorship of books)

Lateran V is sometimes called the council that came too late. It closed on 16 March 1517. On 31 October of the same year, Martin Luther posted his ninety-five theses at Wittenberg. The reform measures Lateran V approved were too modest, too poorly implemented, and arrived after the crisis was already breaking. The lessons of Lateran V helped shape the more thorough reforms of Trent a generation later.
