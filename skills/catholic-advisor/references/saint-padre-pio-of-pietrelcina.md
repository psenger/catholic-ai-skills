# Saint Padre Pio of Pietrelcina (1887 – 1968)

### Saint Padre Pio of Pietrelcina (1887 – 1968)

**Feast day:** September 23 | **Age at death:** 81
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2002 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** Capuchin Franciscan priest; received the stigmata in 1918 (which he bore for fifty years); confessor and spiritual director who reportedly heard ten hours of confessions a day for decades; founder of the Casa Sollievo della Sofferenza (House for the Relief of Suffering), a major hospital at San Giovanni Rotondo

Francesco Forgione joined the Capuchins, took the name Pio, and was ordained in 1910. In 1918 he received the visible stigmata — wounds in his hands, feet, and side that bled, did not heal, and remained for the rest of his life. He underwent intense investigation by Church authorities, including the Holy Office; for a time he was forbidden to celebrate Mass publicly. The investigations were not finally favorable to him until decades later. Through it all he continued to hear confessions, by the testimony of many witnesses with the gift of reading souls. He died in 1968. His stigmata are reported to have closed on the night of his death. He was canonized by John Paul II in 2002.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
