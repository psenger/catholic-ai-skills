# Saint Andrew (d. c. 60–70)

### Saint Andrew (d. c. 60–70)

**Feast day:** November 30 | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** crucified on an X-shaped cross at Patras in Achaea (Greece), tradition; he is said to have requested the diagonal cross because he was unworthy to die as the Lord did
**Symbol in iconography:** the X-shaped (saltire) cross — the cross of Saint Andrew, also the Scottish national flag
**Known for:** first-called (*Protoclete* in the Eastern Church); brother of Peter; apostle to Greece and traditionally to Byzantium

Andrew was originally a disciple of John the Baptist. When John pointed to Jesus and said, "Look, the Lamb of God," Andrew followed him and spent the day with him. Then "he first found his brother Simon and said to him, 'We have found the Messiah'… He brought Simon to Jesus" (John 1:35–42). He is therefore traditionally the "first-called" — the *Protoclete* in Eastern tradition. He is mentioned at the feeding of the five thousand, where it is he who brings the boy with the five loaves and two fish to Jesus (John 6:8–9), and again when certain Greeks come seeking Jesus and Andrew, with Philip, brings them (John 12:20–22). Tradition places his missionary work in Asia Minor and along the Black Sea, with his death by crucifixion at Patras under the proconsul Aegeas. He is the patron saint of Scotland, Russia, Greece, and Romania.
