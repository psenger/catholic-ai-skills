# Saint Justin Martyr (c. 100 – c. 165)

### Saint Justin Martyr (c. 100 – c. 165)

**Era:** Greek (Ante-Nicene) | **Feast day:** June 1 | **Age at death:** approximately 65
**Doctor of the Church:** No
**Major works:** First Apology; Second Apology; Dialogue with Trypho
**Known for:** the first major Christian philosopher; the earliest detailed account of the Christian liturgy outside Scripture; martyred in Rome

Justin was a Greek philosopher who, having tried Stoicism, Aristotelianism, Pythagoreanism, and Platonism, was converted to Christianity by an old man he met on a seashore who pointed him to the Hebrew Scriptures and to the prophets. He kept his philosopher's cloak after his conversion. His First Apology, addressed to the Emperor Antoninus Pius around AD 150, contains the earliest extant detailed description of a Christian Eucharistic liturgy (Apology 65–67) — a description so precise it can be mapped, point by point, onto the structure of the Mass today. He developed the doctrine of the *logos spermatikos* — the seed of the Word — by which he held that whatever truth Plato, Heraclitus, or Socrates grasped was a participation in the Christ who would come. He was beheaded with six companions in Rome under the prefect Rusticus; the trial transcript survives.
