# The North American Martyrs (Saints Isaac Jogues, Jean de Brébeuf, and Companions, d. 1642–1649)

### The North American Martyrs (Saints Isaac Jogues, Jean de Brébeuf, and Companions, d. 1642–1649)

**Feast day:** October 19 | **Ages at death:** Jogues 39; Brébeuf 56
**Canonization status:** Saints (canonized 1930 by Pius XI)
**Known for:** eight French Jesuit missionaries (six priests and two lay brothers) killed by the Iroquois during their mission to the Huron in present-day Canada and upstate New York

Isaac Jogues, a young Jesuit, was captured by the Mohawks in 1642, tortured (his thumbs and fingers were chewed off — incapacitating him, by canon law, for the celebration of Mass; the pope gave him a special dispensation), enslaved for over a year, escaped via Dutch traders to France, and then returned voluntarily to the same Iroquois territory. He was tomahawked at Ossernenon (modern Auriesville, New York) in 1646. Jean de Brébeuf, mission superior at Huronia, was captured during an Iroquois raid in 1649 and tortured to death over hours; the *Relation* by Christophe Regnaut, an eyewitness, describes the torture in unblinking detail. Their courage shaped the Catholic memory of North America.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization documents; Catholic Encyclopedia
