# Blessed Pier Giorgio Frassati (1901 – 1925)

### Blessed Pier Giorgio Frassati (1901 – 1925)

**Feast day:** July 4 | **Age at death:** 24
**Canonization status:** Blessed (beatified 1990 by John Paul II, who called him the "Man of the Beatitudes"; canonization announced for 2025 by Pope Leo XIV)
**Known for:** Italian student of mining engineering; mountaineer; daily communicant; social activist among the urban poor of Turin; member of the Saint Vincent de Paul Society

Pier Giorgio was the son of the founder of the newspaper *La Stampa* and was destined for a comfortable bourgeois future. He spent his student years climbing mountains with friends, organizing political action against fascism, and giving away his money, his clothes, and even his tram fare to the poor of Turin. He contracted polio — probably from a poor man he had visited in his slum — and died within a week, at twenty-four. His parents, who had known little of his hidden charity, were astonished to find the streets of Turin lined with the poor at his funeral. John Paul II beatified him in 1990 and called him *l'uomo delle Beatitudini* — the man of the Beatitudes.

**Sources:** Vatican.va beatification homily
