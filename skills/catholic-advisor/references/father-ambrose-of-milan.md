# Saint Ambrose of Milan (c. 340 – 397)

### Saint Ambrose of Milan (c. 340 – 397)

**Era:** Latin | **Feast day:** December 7 | **Age at death:** approximately 57
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1295 by Boniface VIII as one of the four original Latin Doctors)
**Major works:** *On the Duties of the Clergy* (modeled on Cicero's *De Officiis*); *On the Mysteries*; *On the Sacraments*; many hymns (he is the father of Latin hymnody)
**Known for:** governor of Milan elected bishop by popular acclamation while still a catechumen; the man who baptized Augustine; the bishop who excommunicated and ultimately reconciled the Emperor Theodosius after the massacre at Thessalonica

Ambrose was the Roman governor of Milan when, on the death of the Arian bishop Auxentius in 374, he went to the cathedral to keep the peace at the election of a successor. The crowd cried out for him — *Ambrose for bishop!* — and within a week, having been baptized, ordained, and consecrated bishop, he was leading the see. His preaching was decisive in Augustine's conversion; Augustine's *Confessions* describe his first hearing of Ambrose with admiration that ripened into discipleship (*Confessions* V.13–14). Ambrose's most dramatic act was his confrontation with Theodosius I after the Emperor ordered the massacre of seven thousand citizens of Thessalonica in 390. Ambrose refused Theodosius communion until he performed public penance — and Theodosius submitted. The bishop's spiritual authority over the emperor in matters of moral life became a permanent precedent in Western Christianity.
