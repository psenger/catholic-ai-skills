# Saint Hildegard of Bingen (1098 – 1179)

### Saint Hildegard of Bingen (1098 – 1179)

**Feast day:** September 17 | **Age at death:** 81
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 2012 by Benedict XVI — the first medieval woman Doctor of the Church)
**Known for:** Benedictine abbess of Rupertsberg; visionary mystic; composer (the *Symphonia*); polymath (theology, natural science, medicine); the *Scivias* (her great visionary-theological work)

Hildegard was given to the Benedictine community at Disibodenberg at age eight. She rose to abbess and in middle age began to record the visions she had received since childhood. Her *Scivias* — "Know the Ways" — is a synthetic theological work composed of vision, exegesis, and prophetic exhortation. She wrote on medicine, on natural philosophy, on music; her chants are among the most haunting in the Western tradition. She corresponded with popes, emperors, and bishops. Benedict XVI canonized her by equipollent canonization in 2012 and named her a Doctor of the Church the same year — the first medieval woman in the latter category.

**Sources:** Vatican.va apostolic letter declaring her Doctor (2012)
