# Tertullian (c. 155 – c. 220)

### Tertullian (c. 155 – c. 220)

**Era:** Latin | **Feast day:** none (not canonized) | **Age at death:** approximately 65
**Doctor of the Church:** No (and not canonized — he joined the Montanist sect in his later years and is therefore not formally venerated; he is nevertheless universally classified among the Fathers for his foundational contributions)
**Major works:** *Apology*; *On the Prescription of Heretics*; *Against Marcion*; *On Baptism*; *On the Soul*; *On Patience*; many shorter works
**Known for:** the first major Latin theologian; the man who gave the Latin Church its theological vocabulary, including *Trinitas* (Trinity), *substantia* (substance), *persona* (person) in their technical theological senses

Tertullian was born at Carthage, trained in rhetoric and law, and converted to Christianity around 197. His writings forged the Latin theological language; many of the terms he coined remain in use today. His phrase *credo quia absurdum est* ("I believe because it is absurd") is a paraphrase, not a direct quotation, but it expresses an accurate strand of his thought: the very strangeness of the Incarnation and Resurrection is a sign of their truth, not against it. In his later years he was drawn to Montanism — a charismatic movement that emphasized prophecy, rigorist morality, and the imminence of the end. The Church did not receive Montanism, and Tertullian's adherence to it has kept him out of the Roman Martyrology. He remains, despite this, a Father whose orthodox writings (the bulk of his output) are received as authoritative Catholic witness.
