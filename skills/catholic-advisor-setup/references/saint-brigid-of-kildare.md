# Saint Brigid of Kildare (c. 451 – c. 525)

### Saint Brigid of Kildare (c. 451 – c. 525)

**Feast day:** February 1 | **Age at death:** approximately 74
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** founder of the monastery of Kildare; co-patroness of Ireland (with Patrick and Columba)

Brigid was the daughter of a pagan chieftain and a Christian slave woman. She founded the monastery of Kildare around 480 as a double monastery (men's and women's communities) — a model that would shape Irish monastic Christianity. The historical core of her life is partly obscured by the rich Irish tradition that surrounds her, but the figure is real and the cult is ancient.
