# Saint Aloysius Gonzaga (1568 – 1591)

### Saint Aloysius Gonzaga (1568 – 1591)

**Feast day:** June 21 | **Age at death:** 23
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1726); patron of Catholic youth, of AIDS sufferers and caregivers
**Known for:** Italian prince of the Gonzaga family who renounced his inheritance to join the Jesuits; died nursing victims of the Roman plague of 1591

Aloysius was heir to a marquisate in Lombardy. Against the violent opposition of his father, he renounced his inheritance and joined the Jesuits at seventeen. When the plague struck Rome in 1591, he volunteered to nurse the sick, carrying victims from the streets to the hospitals. He contracted the disease himself and died at twenty-three. He is the patron saint of Catholic youth (Pius XI, 1926) and, by extension, of AIDS caregivers — a patronage proclaimed by John Paul II.
