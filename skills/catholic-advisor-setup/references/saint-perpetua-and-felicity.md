# Saints Perpetua and Felicity (d. 203)

### Saints Perpetua and Felicity (d. 203)

**Feast day:** March 7 | **Ages at death:** Perpetua approximately 22; Felicity unknown (and pregnant in prison)
**Canonization status:** Saints (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** martyred at Carthage under Septimius Severus; the *Passion of Perpetua and Felicity* — partly composed by Perpetua herself, the oldest extant Christian writing by a woman; patrons of mothers and expectant mothers

The *Passion of Saints Perpetua and Felicity* is one of the most precious documents of early Christianity: a personal diary, kept by Perpetua in prison, of her visions, her conversations with her father (who pleaded with her to renounce Christ for the sake of her infant son), and her preparation for death. An eyewitness completed the account after her execution. Perpetua was a young noblewoman with a nursing infant; Felicity was her slave, pregnant in prison and delivered just days before the games (Roman law forbade the execution of a pregnant woman). They were exposed to wild beasts in the arena at Carthage and finished off with the sword. Their names are in the Roman Canon.
