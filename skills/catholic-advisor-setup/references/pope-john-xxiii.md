# Saint John XXIII (1881 – 1963) — Pope 1958–1963

### Saint John XXIII (1881 – 1963) — Pope 1958–1963

**Birth name:** Angelo Giuseppe Roncalli | **Feast day:** October 11 | **Age at death:** 81
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized April 27, 2014, by Pope Francis, alongside John Paul II)
**Known for:** convening the Second Vatican Council (announced 1959, opened 1962); the encyclical *Pacem in Terris* (1963) on peace among all nations; the warmth and humanity of his public image — "Good Pope John"

John XXIII was elected at seventy-seven as what many cardinals expected to be a transitional pope. He announced the Second Vatican Council within three months of his election. *Pacem in Terris* was the first encyclical addressed not only to Catholics but "to all men of good will" — a deliberate gesture of openness to the broader human family. He died of stomach cancer eight months after the close of the Council's first session; Paul VI continued the Council to its conclusion.
