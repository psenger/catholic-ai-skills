# Saint Irenaeus of Lyons (c. 130 – c. 202)

### Saint Irenaeus of Lyons (c. 130 – c. 202)

**Era:** Greek | **Feast day:** June 28 | **Age at death:** approximately 70
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes — proclaimed *Doctor Unitatis* ("Doctor of Unity") by Pope Francis on January 21, 2022
**Major works:** *Against Heresies* (five books); *Demonstration of the Apostolic Preaching*
**Known for:** the first systematic Catholic theologian; the principal patristic refuter of Gnosticism; the founding witness to apostolic succession as a criterion of orthodoxy

Irenaeus was born in Smyrna and as a young man heard Polycarp preach — Polycarp who had heard John the Apostle. He carried that living chain of witness westward to Gaul, where he became bishop of Lyons. The great threat of his age was Gnosticism — an elaborate religious system that claimed secret knowledge passed down outside the public Church. Against it, Irenaeus argued from the visible succession of bishops, traceable in every great see back to the apostles: where the public, public, visible succession of bishops is, there is the truth; where novelty is, there is heresy (Against Heresies III.3). He also developed a theology of *recapitulation* — Christ summing up and reversing the disobedience of Adam in his own obedience, and Mary's *fiat* answering Eve's "no." Pope Francis declared him a Doctor of the Church in 2022 with the title *Doctor Unitatis* in recognition of his witness to the unity of East and West (his name in Greek means "peaceable") and to the unity of Scripture, Tradition, and the apostolic succession.
