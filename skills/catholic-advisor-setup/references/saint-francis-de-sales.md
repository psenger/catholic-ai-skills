# Saint Francis de Sales (1567 – 1622)

### Saint Francis de Sales (1567 – 1622)

**Feast day:** January 24 | **Age at death:** 55
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1877 by Pius IX); *Doctor Caritatis*
**Known for:** the gentle apostle of the Chablais (won back tens of thousands of Calvinists by patient persuasion); bishop of Geneva (resident at Annecy); *Introduction to the Devout Life*; *Treatise on the Love of God*; co-founder (with Jane Frances de Chantal) of the Visitation Sisters

Francis de Sales was born to nobility, trained in law and theology at Padua, and was appointed at twenty-six to lead the Catholic mission in the Chablais — a region just south of Geneva that had become solidly Calvinist. He worked alone for years, often sleeping in barns, until persistence and gentleness began to bear fruit; by the end of his five-year mission, the great majority of the region had returned to communion with Rome. His *Introduction to the Devout Life* (1609) was the first systematic treatise on the spiritual life written for the laity, addressed to a noblewoman who had asked for spiritual direction. He died at Lyon on his way back from preaching the Advent sermons. His phrase "*nothing is so strong as gentleness, nothing so gentle as real strength*" summarizes both his theology and his temperament.
