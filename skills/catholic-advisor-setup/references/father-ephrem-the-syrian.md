# Saint Ephrem the Syrian (c. 306 – 373)

### Saint Ephrem the Syrian (c. 306 – 373)

**Era:** Syriac | **Feast day:** June 9 | **Age at death:** approximately 67
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1920 by Benedict XV — the first Eastern Father proclaimed since the 1568 group; sometimes called *the Harp of the Spirit*)
**Major works:** hundreds of hymns (the *Hymns on Faith*, *Hymns on the Nativity*, *Hymns on Paradise*, *Hymns Against Heresies*); biblical commentaries
**Known for:** the greatest poet of the patristic era; the bridge between Eastern and Western theology; deacon (he refused the priesthood); his hymns shaped Syriac and Armenian liturgy

Ephrem was a deacon of the Church at Nisibis and later at Edessa, writing not in Greek or Latin but in Syriac — a dialect of Aramaic, the language closest to that which Jesus spoke. His hymns are theology in poetry: dense, allusive, biblical, sacramental. He is the principal voice of the Syriac tradition that mediated between the Greek and Latin Fathers and the wider Christian East. Pope Benedict XV proclaimed him a Doctor in 1920, the first Doctor outside the Greek and Latin traditions and a recognition that the Catholic patristic heritage is wider than the Greek-Latin axis.
