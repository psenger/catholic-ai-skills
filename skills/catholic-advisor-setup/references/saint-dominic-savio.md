# Saint Dominic Savio (1842 – 1857)

### Saint Dominic Savio (1842 – 1857)

**Feast day:** March 9 | **Age at death:** 14
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1954 by Pius XII — the youngest non-martyr ever canonized at the time)
**Known for:** pupil of John Bosco at the Oratory of Turin; chosen patron of choristers and of falsely accused persons; the model of the boy striving for holiness in the daily school environment

Dominic was a student at Don Bosco's Oratory who at twelve told the saint: "I would like to be the cloth, and you the tailor." Don Bosco wrote *The Life of Dominic Savio* (1859) as a guide to other young people. His chosen motto was *Death rather than sin*. He famously walked between two boys about to fight with rocks and held up a crucifix, demanding they hit him first. He died of pleurisy at fourteen with the words: "Oh, what beautiful things I see!"

**Sources:** Catholic Encyclopedia entry; Don Bosco's biography
