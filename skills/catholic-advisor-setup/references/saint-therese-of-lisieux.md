# Saint Thérèse of Lisieux (1873 – 1897)

### Saint Thérèse of Lisieux (1873 – 1897)

**Feast day:** October 1 | **Age at death:** 24
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1997 by John Paul II — the third woman Doctor, and the youngest Doctor ever proclaimed)
**Known for:** the "Little Way" of spiritual childhood; *Story of a Soul* (her autobiography, written under obedience); patroness of missions (without ever leaving her convent in France); patron of France with Joan of Arc

Thérèse Martin entered the Carmel of Lisieux in Normandy at fifteen, having obtained the special permission of Pope Leo XIII on a pilgrimage to Rome. She lived there in cloistered obscurity, dying of tuberculosis at twenty-four. Her autobiography *Story of a Soul*, compiled from notebooks she had written under obedience, became one of the most widely read spiritual books of the twentieth century. Her teaching — the "Little Way," the path of trustful spiritual childhood, of doing small things with great love — has shaped countless Catholic lives. She was canonized in 1925, declared patroness of missions in 1927, and proclaimed Doctor of the Church in 1997. Her sister and prioress, Pauline (Mother Agnes of Jesus), preserved and edited her writings.
