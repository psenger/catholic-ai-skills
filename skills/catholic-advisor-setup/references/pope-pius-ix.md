# Blessed Pius IX (1792 – 1878) — Pope 1846–1878

### Blessed Pius IX (1792 – 1878) — Pope 1846–1878

**Birth name:** Giovanni Maria Mastai Ferretti | **Feast day:** February 7 | **Age at death:** 85
**Canonization status:** Blessed (beatified 2000 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** the longest reign in papal history (31 years, 7 months); definition of the dogma of the Immaculate Conception (*Ineffabilis Deus*, 1854); convening of the First Vatican Council (1869–1870); definition of papal infallibility (*Pastor Aeternus*, 1870); the *Syllabus of Errors* (1864); the loss of the Papal States (1870)

Pius IX began his reign as a moderate reformer admired by liberals and ended it as the staunchest defender of Catholic teaching against modern liberalism. The dogma of the Immaculate Conception (the doctrine that Mary, by a singular grace and privilege of God, was preserved from original sin from the first moment of her conception, in view of the merits of Jesus Christ) was the first dogmatic definition by a pope acting on his own authority — a precursor to the First Vatican Council's definition of infallibility. *Pastor Aeternus* (1870) defined papal primacy and papal infallibility. The fall of Rome to the troops of the Kingdom of Italy in September 1870 ended the temporal sovereignty of the popes (the Lateran Treaty of 1929 would resolve the resulting "Roman Question" by creating Vatican City).
