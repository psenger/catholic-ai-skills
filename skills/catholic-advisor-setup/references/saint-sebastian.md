# Saint Sebastian (d. c. 288)

### Saint Sebastian (d. c. 288)

**Feast day:** January 20 | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** Roman officer in the Praetorian Guard, martyred under Diocletian; patron of soldiers, athletes, and (because of the symbolism of his survival of the first attempt on his life) of those who suffer from plague

Sebastian's traditional account (in the *Passion of Saint Sebastian*, fifth century) holds that he was tied to a tree and shot with arrows, was nursed back to health by a Christian widow, and then returned to the palace to confront the emperor, who ordered him beaten to death with cudgels. The historical core (his martyrdom under Diocletian) is attested by Saint Ambrose; the dramatic details may belong to later pious tradition. His name is in the Roman Canon. His iconography — the young man tied to a tree, his body pierced by arrows — is among the most reproduced in Western art.
