# Saint Christopher (d. c. 251, traditional)

### Saint Christopher (d. c. 251, traditional)

**Feast day:** July 25 (reduced to optional local memorial in the universal calendar in 1969) | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation; not removed from sainthood, only from the universal calendar)
**Known for:** the giant who, according to legend, carried the Christ child across a river; patron of travelers, of motorists; widely venerated despite the historical thinness of his account

The historical Christopher — if there was one — is a martyr of the third century named in early martyrologies. The famous legend of his carrying the Child across the river is a later piece of devotional storytelling whose etymological pun (*Christo-phoros*, Christ-bearer) is its theological point. In 1969, the universal calendar was simplified; Christopher's feast was reduced from a universal obligation to an optional local memorial. He was not, contrary to popular reports, "decanonized" — that is not a category in Catholic ecclesiology. He remains a saint of the Church.
