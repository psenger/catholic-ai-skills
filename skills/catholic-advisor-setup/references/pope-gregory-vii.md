# Saint Gregory VII (c. 1015 – 1085) — Pope 1073–1085

### Saint Gregory VII (c. 1015 – 1085) — Pope 1073–1085

**Birth name:** Hildebrand of Sovana | **Feast day:** May 25 | **Age at death:** approximately 70
**Canonization status:** Saint
**Known for:** the Gregorian Reform; the *Dictatus Papae* (1075); the Investiture Controversy; the confrontation with Henry IV at Canossa (January 1077)

Gregory VII was the architect of the Gregorian Reform — the eleventh-century movement to free the Church from lay control over ecclesiastical offices, to enforce celibacy on the clergy, and to suppress simony. His twenty-seven-point *Dictatus Papae* asserted the most far-reaching claims of papal authority then articulated: that the Roman pontiff is the only one whose feet all princes kiss; that he can depose emperors; that the Roman Church has never erred. The struggle with Emperor Henry IV culminated in Henry's three days of penance, barefoot in the snow, outside Gregory's host's castle at Canossa in January 1077. Gregory died in exile at Salerno five years later with the words: "I have loved justice and hated iniquity; therefore I die in exile."
