# Benedict XVI (1927 – 2022) — Pope 2005–2013

### Benedict XVI (1927 – 2022) — Pope 2005–2013

**Birth name:** Joseph Aloisius Ratzinger | **Age at death:** 95
**Canonization status:** not under cause
**Known for:** the first pope to resign voluntarily since Celestine V in 1294 (February 28, 2013); the encyclicals *Deus Caritas Est* (2005), *Spe Salvi* (2007), *Caritas in Veritate* (2009); the three-volume *Jesus of Nazareth* (2007–2012); the implementation of *Summorum Pontificum* (2007) restoring wider use of the 1962 Roman Missal; the 2005 Christmas address on the "hermeneutic of reform in continuity" as the right interpretation of Vatican II

Benedict XVI was a peritus at Vatican II as a young theologian, a professor at Tübingen, Bonn, and Regensburg, archbishop of Munich (1977), and Prefect of the Congregation for the Doctrine of the Faith under John Paul II for over twenty-three years before his election as pope at seventy-eight. His pontificate was marked by a serene, theologically dense approach. His resignation, announced on February 11, 2013, citing the diminished strength of body and mind that an "advanced age" had brought him, was the first papal resignation in seven centuries. He lived as Pope Emeritus at the Mater Ecclesiae monastery in Vatican City until his death on December 31, 2022.
