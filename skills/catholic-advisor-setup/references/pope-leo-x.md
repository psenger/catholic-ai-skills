# Leo X (1475 – 1521) — Pope 1513–1521

### Leo X (1475 – 1521) — Pope 1513–1521

**Birth name:** Giovanni de' Medici | **Age at death:** approximately 45
**Canonization status:** not canonized
**Known for:** the pope who excommunicated Martin Luther (*Exsurge Domine* 1520; *Decet Romanum Pontificem* 1521); a great patron of Renaissance art and learning; presided over the Fifth Lateran Council

The son of Lorenzo the Magnificent, Leo X was made a cardinal at thirteen and pope at thirty-seven. He continued the building of Saint Peter's Basilica and was a patron of Raphael and many other artists. The financing of these projects through the controversial sale of indulgences — particularly the indulgence preached by Tetzel in Germany — was the immediate trigger for Luther's Ninety-Five Theses (1517). Leo X's bulls condemning Luther's positions on grace, the sacraments, and papal authority are foundational documents of the Catholic side of the Reformation.
