# Saint Joseph (1st century BC – 1st century AD)

### Saint Joseph (1st century BC – 1st century AD)

**Feast days:** Solemnity of Saint Joseph (March 19); Saint Joseph the Worker (May 1) | **Age at death:** unknown (Tradition holds he died before Christ's public ministry, since he is not named at the Cross)
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation; declared Patron of the Universal Church by Blessed Pius IX in 1870)
**Known for:** the husband of Mary; the foster-father of Jesus; patron of the Universal Church; patron of workers, fathers, and a holy death

Joseph appears in the infancy narratives of Matthew (chapters 1–2) and Luke (chapters 1–2) and only by inference thereafter. He is the "just man" (Matthew 1:19) who took Mary into his home when an angel revealed to him in a dream that the child she carried was conceived by the Holy Spirit, who fled with the family to Egypt to save the child from Herod, who returned to Nazareth, and who taught Jesus the trade of a *tekton* — a builder or carpenter (Mark 6:3). He is silent in the Gospels: no word attributed to him is recorded. Pope Pius IX declared him Patron of the Universal Church in 1870. Pope Francis dedicated the year 2020–2021 to him with the apostolic letter *Patris Corde*. He is the model of the head of a family, of the working man, and of the holy death (tradition holds that he died with Mary and Jesus at his side, the model of Christian dying).
