# 11. Third Council of the Lateran (1179)

### 11. Third Council of the Lateran (1179)

**Convoked by:** Pope Alexander III
**Issue addressed:** electoral reform of the papacy after another schism
**Key teachings:**
- Required a two-thirds majority of cardinals for a valid papal election — the rule that still governs conclaves today
- Disciplinary canons against heresy, usury, and clerical abuses
- The first conciliar legislation against Cathar (Albigensian) heresy

Lateran III's electoral reform is among the most consequential acts in Catholic history: the two-thirds rule, with minor modifications, has governed every papal election since. The conclave of 2025 that elected Pope Leo XIV followed this principle.
