# Saint Bernard of Clairvaux (1090 – 1153)

### Saint Bernard of Clairvaux (1090 – 1153)

**Feast day:** August 20 | **Age at death:** 63
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1830 by Pius VIII); *Doctor Mellifluus* ("the Honey-flowing Doctor")
**Known for:** the great reformer of the Cistercian Order; the preacher of the Second Crusade (1146); spiritual director of popes, kings, and bishops; *On Loving God*; the *Sermons on the Song of Songs*

Bernard entered the new Cistercian monastery of Cîteaux in 1112 with thirty companions, and three years later he was sent to found the abbey of Clairvaux — which under his abbacy became the mother of dozens of daughter houses. He was the preeminent spiritual voice of his century. His preaching of the Second Crusade was widely heard; the Crusade's failure was a sorrow he himself acknowledged. His writings — *On Loving God*, his sermons on the Song of Songs, his treatise on grace and free will — are masterpieces of medieval monastic theology.
