# Blessed Charles de Foucauld (1858 – 1916)

### Blessed Charles de Foucauld (1858 – 1916)

**Feast day:** December 1 | **Age at death:** 58
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2022 by Pope Francis)
**Known for:** former French army officer and explorer; converted from atheism in 1886; lived as a hermit and priest among the Tuareg of the Algerian Sahara; killed by anti-colonial raiders in 1916

Charles de Foucauld was a wealthy, dissolute French aristocrat and soldier who explored Morocco disguised as a Jewish rabbi in 1883–1884. The hospitality he received from Muslim families astonished him. Back in France, he converted to Catholicism in 1886 — "As soon as I believed there was a God, I understood that I could not do otherwise than live for him alone." He became a Trappist, then a hermit, and finally, ordained at forty-three, he settled at Tamanrasset in the Sahara among the Tuareg. He intended to found a community of "Little Brothers" but had no followers in his lifetime. He was shot by raiders in 1916 at fifty-eight. The communities he had envisioned — the Little Brothers and Little Sisters of Jesus — were founded after his death and continue today. He was canonized in 2022.
