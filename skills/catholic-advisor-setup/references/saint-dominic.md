# Saint Dominic (1170 – 1221)

### Saint Dominic (1170 – 1221)

**Feast day:** August 8 | **Age at death:** approximately 51
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1234 by Gregory IX)
**Known for:** founder of the Order of Preachers (Dominicans, 1216); preached the Rosary in southern France against the Albigensian heresy (traditional, though the actual development of the Rosary is later)

Dominic was a Spanish canon of Osma who, traveling with his bishop through southern France around 1203, encountered the Albigensian heresy and devoted the rest of his life to its refutation through preaching and education. His Order — the Order of Preachers — was approved in 1216 and became one of the two great mendicant orders of the medieval Church (the Franciscans being the other). The Dominicans became the principal Catholic theological order: Thomas Aquinas, Albert the Great, Catherine of Siena, Bartolomé de las Casas, and many others were Dominicans.
