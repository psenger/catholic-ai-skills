# Saint Scholastica (c. 480 – c. 543)

### Saint Scholastica (c. 480 – c. 543)

**Feast day:** February 10 | **Age at death:** approximately 63
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** twin sister of Benedict; the founder of Benedictine women's monasticism

Almost everything we know of Scholastica comes from Book II of Gregory the Great's *Dialogues*. She founded a women's community some five miles from Monte Cassino, and the twins met once a year for spiritual conversation. The most famous story — that on one such meeting, she begged Benedict to remain longer, was refused, prayed, and produced a storm that compelled him to stay — is recorded by Gregory and reflects the Benedictine tradition that charity outranks discipline. Three days after that meeting, Scholastica died; Benedict had her body brought to Monte Cassino, and when he died, they were buried in the same grave.
