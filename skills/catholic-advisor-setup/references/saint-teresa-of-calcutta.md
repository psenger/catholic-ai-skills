# Saint Teresa of Calcutta (1910 – 1997)

### Saint Teresa of Calcutta (1910 – 1997)

**Feast day:** September 5 | **Age at death:** 87
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2016 by Pope Francis); Nobel Peace Prize (1979)
**Known for:** founder of the Missionaries of Charity (1950); apostle of the poorest of the poor in Calcutta and beyond; the modern face of Catholic charity

Anjezë Bojaxhiu was born in Skopje (now North Macedonia), entered the Loreto Sisters at eighteen, and was sent to teach in Calcutta. In 1946, on a train to Darjeeling, she received what she called the "call within the call": to leave the Loreto and to serve "the poorest of the poor, the unwanted, the unloved, the uncared for." She received permission in 1948 and went into the slums of Calcutta in a sari. The first Missionaries of Charity were her own former students. She received the Nobel Peace Prize in 1979; she gave the prize money to the poor and asked the Norwegian government to cancel the prize banquet and give that money to the poor as well. Her Missionaries of Charity number some six thousand sisters and brothers in over 130 countries. Her letters, published after her death, revealed that for the last decades of her life she experienced a profound spiritual darkness — the night of the senses that John of the Cross described — without consolation but in continuing fidelity. She is the modern saint of the corporal works of mercy and of perseverance in faith without felt comfort.
