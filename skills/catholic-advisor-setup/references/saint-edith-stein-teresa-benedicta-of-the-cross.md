# Saint Edith Stein / Teresa Benedicta of the Cross (1891 – 1942)

### Saint Edith Stein / Teresa Benedicta of the Cross (1891 – 1942)

**Feast day:** August 9 | **Age at death:** 50
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1998 by John Paul II); co-patroness of Europe (with Catherine of Siena and Bridget of Sweden, named 1999)
**Known for:** German Jewish philosopher, student of Edmund Husserl; convert to Catholicism (1922) after reading the autobiography of Teresa of Ávila; Discalced Carmelite nun; killed in the gas chambers of Auschwitz

Edith Stein was a brilliant young German Jew, one of the first women in Germany to receive a doctorate in philosophy. She was the favorite student and chief assistant of Husserl, the founder of phenomenology. Her conversion to Catholicism came in 1922 after a sleepless night reading Teresa of Ávila's autobiography — at dawn she said: "This is the truth." She taught for a decade in Catholic schools, was forced out by the Nazi racial laws in 1933, and entered the Carmel of Cologne. To escape the Nazis, she was transferred to the Carmel of Echt in the Netherlands. When the Dutch bishops condemned Nazi anti-Semitism in July 1942, the Gestapo retaliated by arresting all baptized Jews in Holland. Edith and her sister Rosa (also a convert) were arrested on August 2, 1942, and gassed at Auschwitz on August 9. As she was led from the convent, Edith took Rosa's hand and said: "Come, we are going for our people."
