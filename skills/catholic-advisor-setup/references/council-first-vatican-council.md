# 20. First Vatican Council (1869–1870)

### 20. First Vatican Council (1869–1870)

**Convoked by:** Pope Pius IX | **Interrupted by:** the Franco-Prussian War and the fall of Rome to Italian unification troops, before the council could complete its planned agenda
**Issue addressed:** the relationship of faith and reason in the modern world, and the authority of the Roman Pontiff
**Key teachings:**
- *Dei Filius* (1870): the dogmatic constitution on Catholic faith, treating revelation, faith, the relationship of faith and reason, and the impossibility of any real contradiction between them
- *Pastor Aeternus* (1870): the dogmatic constitution on the Church of Christ — defined papal primacy (the pope has full and universal jurisdiction over the Catholic Church) and papal infallibility (when the pope, as supreme pastor of the universal Church, definitively proclaims a doctrine concerning faith or morals, he is preserved from error by the assistance of the Holy Spirit, *ex cathedra*)

The council was interrupted by political crisis after promulgating *Pastor Aeternus*. The remaining schemata — including a treatment of the episcopate that would have balanced *Pastor Aeternus* with a full account of the role of the bishops — were never voted on. The balance was supplied a century later by *Lumen Gentium* at Vatican II. The definition of infallibility is widely misunderstood; it does not declare the pope incapable of personal sin or theological error in everyday matters, but only that when he definitively defines doctrine concerning faith or morals, in his role as universal pastor, he is preserved from defining error. The conditions are stringent and the exercise is rare.
