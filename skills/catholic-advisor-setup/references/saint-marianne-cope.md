# Saint Marianne Cope (1838 – 1918)

### Saint Marianne Cope (1838 – 1918)

**Feast day:** January 23 | **Age at death:** 80
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2012 by Benedict XVI)
**Known for:** Franciscan sister of Syracuse, New York, who led six other sisters to Hawaii in 1883 to serve patients with Hansen's disease; took over the women's section of Damien's mission at Kalaupapa after his death

Marianne Cope was a German immigrant who joined the Franciscan Sisters of Syracuse. As Provincial Superior, she received in 1883 a request from the Hawaiian government, which had been refused by some fifty other religious congregations, to staff a hospital for Hansen's disease patients in Honolulu. She accepted personally — "I am hungry for the work and I wish with all my heart to be one of the chosen ones, whose privilege it will be to sacrifice themselves for the salvation of the souls of the poor Islanders." She went with six sisters and remained the rest of her life. After Damien's death she took over the women's section of the Kalaupapa colony, where she remained until her own death at eighty.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
