# Saint George (d. c. 303)

### Saint George (d. c. 303)

**Feast day:** April 23 | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** Roman soldier, martyred under Diocletian; the dragon-slayer (legend, not history); patron of England, of soldiers, of Boy Scouts

The historical kernel — George was a Christian soldier of the Roman army, martyred under Diocletian, probably at Nicomedia or Lydda — is solid. Everything beyond that, including the famous dragon story (which entered Western tradition in the late medieval *Golden Legend*), is pious legend. The cult is nevertheless ancient (his church at Lydda is mentioned in the fourth century) and well-established. He is the patron of England and is held in particular honor in Eastern Christianity.
