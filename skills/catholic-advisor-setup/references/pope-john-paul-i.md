# John Paul I (1912 – 1978) — Pope August 26–September 28, 1978

### John Paul I (1912 – 1978) — Pope August 26–September 28, 1978

**Birth name:** Albino Luciani | **Age at death:** 65
**Canonization status:** Blessed (beatified September 4, 2022, by Pope Francis)
**Known for:** the thirty-three-day pope; the "September Pope"; the first pope to take a double name (in honor of John XXIII and Paul VI); the first pope to drop the coronation in favor of an inauguration Mass

John Paul I was found dead in his bedroom in the early morning of September 29, 1978, having been pope just thirty-three days. The cause of death was a heart attack. Conspiracy theories surrounding his death have been thoroughly investigated and dismissed by serious historians and by Vatican investigators. He was beatified in 2022. His warmth, his humility, and the simplicity of his teaching style left a deep impression in his brief pontificate.
