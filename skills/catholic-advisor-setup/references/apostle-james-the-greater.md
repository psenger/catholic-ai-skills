# Saint James the Greater (d. 44)

### Saint James the Greater (d. 44)

**Feast day:** July 25 | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** beheaded in Jerusalem by Herod Agrippa I in 44 — the only apostle whose death is recorded in Scripture (Acts 12:1–2)
**Symbol in iconography:** scallop shell; pilgrim's staff and hat; sword
**Known for:** first apostle martyred; one of the inner three (Peter, James, John); patron of Spain (Santiago de Compostela)

James the son of Zebedee and brother of John (called "the Greater" to distinguish him from the other James) was called with his brother while mending nets with their father (Matthew 4:21–22). Jesus gave the two brothers the name *Boanerges*, "sons of thunder" (Mark 3:17). James belonged with Peter and John to the inner three apostles who alone witnessed the Transfiguration (Matthew 17:1–8), the raising of Jairus's daughter (Mark 5:37), and the Agony in Gethsemane (Mark 14:33). He and John once asked to sit at Christ's right and left in the kingdom and were warned that they would drink the cup that Christ would drink (Mark 10:35–40) — a prophecy fulfilled at his beheading. Acts records: "About that time King Herod laid violent hands upon some who belonged to the church. He had James, the brother of John, killed with the sword" (Acts 12:1–2). The tradition that James first preached the gospel in Spain, then returned to Jerusalem to be martyred, gives rise to the great pilgrimage to his shrine at Compostela.
