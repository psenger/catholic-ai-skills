# Stephen II (d. 757) — Pope 752–757

### Stephen II (d. 757) — Pope 752–757

**Birth name:** Stephen | **Feast day:** not in the universal calendar | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** not formally canonized; venerated
**Known for:** the Donation of Pepin (754) — the act that created the Papal States

Stephen II's pontificate transformed the political position of the Roman bishop. With the Lombards pressing Rome, Stephen traveled across the Alps in winter — the first pope ever to do so — to seek aid from Pepin the Short, the Frankish king. Pepin agreed, defeated the Lombards, and "donated" to the pope a wide swath of central Italy. The Papal States, in some form, remained until 1870. The political and theological consequences — the popes as temporal sovereigns — were long-lasting.
