# Saint Lucy of Syracuse (c. 283 – 304)

### Saint Lucy of Syracuse (c. 283 – 304)

**Feast day:** December 13 | **Age at death:** approximately 21
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** martyred at Syracuse in Sicily during the Diocletianic persecution; in iconography, holding a plate with her eyes (the traditional account says her eyes were torn out before her execution); patroness of the blind, of writers, and of those with eye diseases

Lucy is named in the Roman Canon among the early Roman and Sicilian martyrs. The historical core (her death under Diocletian at Syracuse) is well attested; the details, including the legend of the eyes, are later. Her name (Latin *lux*, light) and her feast date — close to the winter solstice in the old Julian calendar — combined to make her the great saint of light in Northern European tradition, especially in Sweden, where her feast is celebrated with candles and processions.
