# Gregory IX (c. 1145 – 1241) — Pope 1227–1241

### Gregory IX (c. 1145 – 1241) — Pope 1227–1241

**Birth name:** Ugolino di Conti | **Age at death:** approximately 96
**Canonization status:** not canonized
**Known for:** canonized his friend Francis of Assisi in 1228 (two years after Francis's death); established the *Decretals of Gregory IX* (1234), the foundational compilation of medieval canon law; institutionalized the papal Inquisition

Gregory IX's *Decretals*, compiled by Raymond of Peñafort, remained the working canon law of the Catholic Church until the 1917 Code. The papal Inquisition he established — a tribunal under papal rather than local bishop authority — was directed primarily against the Cathars and other doctrinal dissenters. The historical assessment of the Inquisition has been the subject of major Catholic scholarly and magisterial reflection, including the document of the International Theological Commission *Memory and Reconciliation* (2000) and John Paul II's request for forgiveness for the wrongs done in the name of the Inquisition.
