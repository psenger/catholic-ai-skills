# Pius XI (1857 – 1939) — Pope 1922–1939

### Pius XI (1857 – 1939) — Pope 1922–1939

**Birth name:** Achille Ratti | **Age at death:** 81
**Canonization status:** not under cause
**Known for:** the Lateran Treaty (1929) resolving the "Roman Question" and creating Vatican City State; *Quadragesimo Anno* (1931) developing Catholic social doctrine; *Casti Connubii* (1930) on Christian marriage; *Mit Brennender Sorge* (1937) against Nazi racism (in German, smuggled into Germany and read from every pulpit on Palm Sunday)

Pius XI was a scholar and Vatican librarian before becoming pope. His Lateran Treaty with Mussolini's Italy (1929) ended the fifty-nine-year stand-off between the Holy See and the Kingdom of Italy that had begun with the loss of the Papal States. *Casti Connubii* re-affirmed the indissolubility of Christian marriage and condemned contraception. *Mit Brennender Sorge*, drafted in part by the future Pius XII, condemned Nazi racial ideology with unusual directness; that it was written and circulated in German, not Latin, made its target unmistakable. Pius XI also condemned atheistic Communism in *Divini Redemptoris* (1937).
