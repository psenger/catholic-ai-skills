# Saint Josephine Bakhita (c. 1869 – 1947)

### Saint Josephine Bakhita (c. 1869 – 1947)

**Feast day:** February 8 | **Age at death:** approximately 78
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2000 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** Sudanese Canossian sister; sold into slavery at age nine, eventually freed in Italy, freely embraced the Catholic faith and religious life; patron of the Sudan and of trafficking victims

Bakhita was kidnapped from her family in Darfur as a girl, sold and resold five times across the slave markets of North Africa, beaten and tattooed. Eventually purchased by an Italian diplomat and brought to Italy, she was entrusted to the Canossian Sisters in Venice, where she came to know the Christian faith. When her owner came to take her back, Italian law (which did not recognize slavery on Italian soil) freed her. She chose to be baptized, took the name Josephine, and entered the Canossian Sisters in 1893. She lived a hidden life as the porter and cook of her community for fifty-four years. She died at seventy-eight. Pope Benedict XVI cited her at length in *Spe Salvi* as a witness to Christian hope.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
