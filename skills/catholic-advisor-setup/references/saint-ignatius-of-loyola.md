# Saint Ignatius of Loyola (1491 – 1556)

### Saint Ignatius of Loyola (1491 – 1556)

**Feast day:** July 31 | **Age at death:** 65
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1622 by Gregory XV, with Francis Xavier and Teresa of Ávila)
**Known for:** founder of the Society of Jesus (Jesuits, approved 1540); the *Spiritual Exercises*; *ad maiorem Dei gloriam* — "for the greater glory of God"

Ignatius was a Basque soldier whose leg was shattered by a cannonball at the siege of Pamplona (1521). Bedridden during his long convalescence, he asked for romances of chivalry; finding only a Life of Christ and lives of the saints, he read those instead and was converted. He withdrew to a cave at Manresa for nearly a year of prayer, where the substance of the *Spiritual Exercises* — a structured month-long retreat of meditation and discernment — was given him. He studied at Paris with the small group that would become the first Jesuits, and the Society of Jesus was approved by Pope Paul III in 1540. By his death sixteen years later, the Jesuits numbered over a thousand, and they became the principal missionary, educational, and intellectual force of the Counter-Reformation.
