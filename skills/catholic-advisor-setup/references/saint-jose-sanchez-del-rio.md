# Saint José Sánchez del Río (1913 – 1928)

### Saint José Sánchez del Río (1913 – 1928)

**Feast day:** February 10 | **Age at death:** 14
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2016 by Francis)
**Known for:** Mexican boy martyr of the Cristero War; tortured and killed for refusing to deny Christ during the Mexican government's persecution of the Catholic Church

During the Cristero rebellion (1926–1929) against the Calles government's anticlerical laws, José ran away to join the Cristero forces despite being only thirteen. Captured, he was tortured for days — the soles of his feet flayed — to force him to renounce his faith. He refused. He was made to walk to the cemetery, where he was bayoneted and finally shot, dying with the words *Viva Cristo Rey* — "Long live Christ the King" — on his lips. He was fourteen.
