# Saint Cecilia (c. 200, traditional dates uncertain)

### Saint Cecilia (c. 200, traditional dates uncertain)

**Feast day:** November 22 | **Age at death:** unknown
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** patroness of music and musicians; the bride who converted her husband Valerian on their wedding night

The earliest accounts of Cecilia's martyrdom date from the fifth century and contain much pious legend; the historical core is uncertain. The cult is nevertheless ancient — her name is in the Roman Canon — and her tomb in the Catacomb of Saint Callixtus has been excavated. Her association with music dates from a misreading of the traditional account: at her wedding feast, the legend says, while the instruments played, she sang in her heart to God alone. Cardinal Sfondrati examined her tomb in 1599 and found her body apparently incorrupt; the sculptor Stefano Maderno recorded the position in his famous statue at Trastevere.
