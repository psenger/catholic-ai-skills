# Saint Philip (d. c. 80)

### Saint Philip (d. c. 80)

**Feast day:** May 3 (with James the Less) | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** crucified or stoned at Hierapolis in Phrygia (traditional)
**Symbol in iconography:** loaves of bread (his question at the feeding of the five thousand); a tall cross
**Known for:** "Show us the Father" — his request at the Last Supper that prompted one of Christ's deepest self-revelations

Philip was from Bethsaida, the town of Andrew and Peter (John 1:44). He was called personally by Jesus — "Follow me" — and immediately found Nathanael (commonly identified as Bartholomew) and brought him to Christ with the words, "We have found him about whom Moses in the law and also the prophets wrote, Jesus son of Joseph from Nazareth" (John 1:43–46). At the feeding of the five thousand, Jesus tested him: "Where are we to buy bread for these people to eat?" — and Philip replied with mathematical realism, "Six months' wages would not buy enough bread for each of them to get a little" (John 6:5–7). It was through Philip that certain Greeks came to Jesus before the Passion (John 12:20–22). His most enduring moment is the Last Supper: "Lord, show us the Father, and we will be satisfied" — to which Jesus replied, "Whoever has seen me has seen the Father" (John 14:8–9). Tradition gives him a mission in Phrygia and a martyr's death at Hierapolis.
