# Saint John Paul II (1920 – 2005) — Pope 1978–2005

### Saint John Paul II (1920 – 2005) — Pope 1978–2005

**Birth name:** Karol Józef Wojtyła | **Feast day:** October 22 | **Age at death:** 84
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized April 27, 2014, by Pope Francis)
**Known for:** the longest reign of the modern era (26 years, 5 months); the first non-Italian pope since Adrian VI (1522); the role of his pontificate in the collapse of Communism in Eastern Europe; the canonization of 482 saints (more than all his predecessors combined); fourteen encyclicals including *Redemptor Hominis* (1979), *Veritatis Splendor* (1993), *Evangelium Vitae* (1995), *Fides et Ratio* (1998); the Theology of the Body (a series of 129 General Audience addresses, 1979–1984); World Youth Day; the Catechism of the Catholic Church (1992); the Code of Canon Law revision (1983); 104 pastoral visits to 129 countries

John Paul II was born in Poland, lost his mother at nine, his father at twenty-one, and his only brother before that. He studied for the priesthood clandestinely under the Nazi occupation, was ordained in 1946, became a parish priest, a professor of philosophy, an auxiliary bishop of Kraków at thirty-eight, archbishop of Kraków at forty-four, a cardinal at forty-seven, and pope at fifty-eight. The assassination attempt against him in Saint Peter's Square on May 13, 1981, did not interrupt his pontificate; he forgave the gunman, Mehmet Ali Ağca, and visited him in prison. His pastoral teaching on the human person, the family, the dignity of work, the role of women, the meaning of suffering, the Gospel of Life, and the unity of Christians spans a vast corpus. His Theology of the Body is one of the most original theological developments of the late twentieth century. He died on April 2, 2005, with crowds in Saint Peter's Square chanting *santo subito* — "a saint at once."
