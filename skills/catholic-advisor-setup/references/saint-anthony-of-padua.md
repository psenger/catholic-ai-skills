# Saint Anthony of Padua (1195 – 1231)

### Saint Anthony of Padua (1195 – 1231)

**Feast day:** June 13 | **Age at death:** 35
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1946 by Pius XII); *Doctor Evangelicus*
**Known for:** the Franciscan preacher *par excellence*; patron of lost things; an Augustinian Canon before joining the early Franciscan movement

Anthony was born in Lisbon (Portugal) into a noble family, joined the Augustinian Canons, and later — moved by the witness of five Franciscan martyrs whose remains had been returned from Morocco — transferred to the new Order of Friars Minor (Franciscans). He was sent to preach against the Cathar heresy in southern France and northern Italy; his learning, eloquence, and personal holiness made him the most sought-after preacher of his day. He died at thirty-five at Padua. He was canonized within a year — one of the fastest canonizations in Catholic history — and named a Doctor of the Church seven centuries later.
