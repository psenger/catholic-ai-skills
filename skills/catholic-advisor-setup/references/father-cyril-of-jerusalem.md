# Saint Cyril of Jerusalem (c. 313 – 386)

### Saint Cyril of Jerusalem (c. 313 – 386)

**Era:** Greek | **Feast day:** March 18 | **Age at death:** approximately 73
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1882 by Leo XIII)
**Major works:** *Catechetical Lectures* (twenty-three lectures delivered in Jerusalem to those preparing for baptism and to the newly baptized — the *Mystagogical Catecheses*)
**Known for:** the patriarch of catechetical literature; the most detailed early witness to Christian initiation and the Eucharist

The *Catechetical Lectures* are the most complete record of fourth-century Christian initiation we possess. The first eighteen, addressed to candidates for baptism, expound the Creed article by article. The final five — the *Mystagogical Catecheses* — were delivered after baptism to the newly initiated and explain the rites of baptism, chrismation (confirmation), and the Eucharist. The latter are among the strongest patristic witnesses to the Real Presence: "Since then he himself has declared and said of the bread, 'This is my Body,' who shall dare to doubt any longer? And since he has affirmed and said, 'This is my Blood,' who shall ever hesitate, saying that it is not his Blood?" (*Mystagogical Catechesis* IV.1). Cyril was exiled three times by Arian opponents but lived to see the triumph of Nicene orthodoxy at Constantinople I, where he was present.
