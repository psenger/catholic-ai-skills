# Mary, the Mother of God (1st century BC – 1st century AD)

### Mary, the Mother of God (1st century BC – 1st century AD)

**Feast days:** Solemnity of Mary, Mother of God (January 1); Immaculate Conception (December 8); Annunciation (March 25); Visitation (May 31); Nativity of the Blessed Virgin (September 8); Our Lady of Sorrows (September 15); Queenship of Mary (August 22); Assumption (August 15); plus many other Marian feasts — Our Lady of the Rosary (October 7), Our Lady of Guadalupe (December 12), Our Lady of Lourdes (February 11), Our Lady of Fatima (May 13), etc.
**Title:** *Theotokos* (Mother of God, defined at Ephesus 431); Immaculate Conception (defined 1854); Assumption (defined 1950)
**Known for:** the Mother of God; the perfect disciple; the Queen of all saints

Mary is honored above all the saints — neither as God nor as a goddess, but as the human creature most perfectly conformed to her Son. The four Marian dogmas — the perpetual virginity, the divine motherhood (*Theotokos*), the Immaculate Conception, and the bodily Assumption — are not Catholic additions to the Gospel but the Church's deepening grasp of what is already implicit in Scripture. Catholic veneration of Mary is *hyperdoulia* — a higher honor than that paid to other saints (*doulia*) but categorically lower than the adoration owed to God alone (*latria*). The Catechism teaches that Mary is the Mother of the Church (CCC 963–975). Marian devotion takes many forms: the Rosary, the Hail Mary, the Litany of Loreto, the Memorare, the Angelus, the seasonal cycle of Marian antiphons (Alma Redemptoris Mater, Ave Regina Caelorum, Regina Caeli, Salve Regina), and the great approved Marian apparitions (Guadalupe 1531, Lourdes 1858, Fatima 1917, among others).
