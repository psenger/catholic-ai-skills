# Saint Bartholomew (d. c. 1st century)

### Saint Bartholomew (d. c. 1st century)

**Feast day:** August 24 | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** flayed alive and beheaded in Armenia (traditional; depicted by Michelangelo in the Sistine Chapel holding his own skin)
**Symbol in iconography:** a flaying knife; his own skin held in his hand
**Known for:** likely identical with Nathanael of John 1; the apostle in whom Jesus saw "no deceit"

Bartholomew (meaning "son of Tolmai") is named in all three Synoptic lists of the Twelve (Matthew 10:3; Mark 3:18; Luke 6:14) and in Acts 1:13, but the Gospel of John does not list a Bartholomew. John instead describes Nathanael of Cana in Galilee, brought to Jesus by Philip (John 1:45–51). Since Bartholomew is paired with Philip in the Synoptic lists, and since Nathanael is paired with Philip in John, the Church's tradition (as old as the ninth century) has identified them as the same man. When Philip introduces Nathanael to Jesus, Jesus says: "Here is truly an Israelite in whom there is no deceit" (John 1:47). Nathanael protests that he was sitting under a fig tree when no one could have seen him, and Jesus replies that he saw him under the fig tree — to which Nathanael confesses: "Rabbi, you are the Son of God! You are the King of Israel!" (John 1:49). Tradition gives him a mission in India and Armenia, where he is venerated as the apostle who first brought the gospel; he is said to have been martyred by flaying. He is the patron of the Armenian Apostolic Church.
