# 13. First Council of Lyon (1245)

### 13. First Council of Lyon (1245)

**Convoked by:** Pope Innocent IV
**Issue addressed:** the conflict between the papacy and the Hohenstaufen Emperor Frederick II
**Key teachings:**
- Deposed Frederick II
- Authorized the use of the red cardinal's hat — a small but lasting disciplinary mark

The council's main act — the papal deposition of an emperor — illustrates the medieval theory of the *plenitudo potestatis*, the fullness of papal power over both spiritual and temporal authority. The historical effect was limited; Frederick continued to reign until his death in 1250. The political theology behind such acts was significantly qualified at Vatican II's *Dignitatis Humanae* (1965), which affirmed the proper autonomy of the temporal order and religious liberty.
