# Saint Lawrence of Brindisi (1559 – 1619)

### Saint Lawrence of Brindisi (1559 – 1619)

**Feast day:** July 21 | **Age at death:** 60
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1959 by John XXIII); *Doctor Apostolicus*
**Known for:** Capuchin Franciscan; Minister General of the Capuchins; preacher, diplomat, and military chaplain (he led an army into battle against the Turks in 1601 carrying only a crucifix)

Lawrence's gift for languages — he mastered Hebrew, Greek, Latin, Italian, Spanish, French, German, and Czech — made him the Vatican's principal diplomat for half a century, sent on missions to Protestant Germany, to the imperial court, to Bavaria, to Munich, to Spain. His preaching to Jewish communities was respectful and learned (he preached in Hebrew). He died at sixty on a diplomatic mission to King Philip III at Lisbon.
