# Saint Cyril of Alexandria (c. 376 – 444)

### Saint Cyril of Alexandria (c. 376 – 444)

**Era:** Greek (Alexandrian) | **Feast day:** June 27 | **Age at death:** approximately 68
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1882 by Leo XIII)
**Major works:** *On the Unity of Christ*; *Commentary on John*; *That Christ is One*; the twelve anathemas against Nestorius
**Known for:** the architect of the Council of Ephesus (431); the champion of *Theotokos*; the principal patristic Christologist

Cyril succeeded his uncle Theophilus as Patriarch of Alexandria in 412. His confrontation with Nestorius of Constantinople over the title *Theotokos* — Mother of God — set the agenda of the Council of Ephesus (431), where Cyril presided in the name of Pope Celestine I. His twelve anathemas against Nestorius are among the most precise christological texts of the patristic age. Cyril's actions in Alexandria — including the closure of the city's Jewish quarter and his role in the period of unrest that led to the murder of the philosopher Hypatia in 415 — are part of his historical record and rightly subject to criticism. His doctrinal work, however, is the indispensable Christological foundation on which Chalcedon was built. He insisted, against Nestorius, that there is one subject in Christ — the divine Word made flesh — and that Mary therefore truly is Mother of God.
