# 12. Fourth Council of the Lateran (1215)

### 12. Fourth Council of the Lateran (1215)

**Convoked by:** Pope Innocent III | **Bishops present:** over 400, plus 800 abbots and priors, with representatives of every major Christian sovereign
**Issue addressed:** the high-water mark of the medieval papacy — Innocent III's comprehensive program of Church reform and the response to the Cathar heresy
**Key teachings:**
- The first conciliar use of the word *transubstantiatio* (transubstantiation) to describe the change of bread and wine into the Body and Blood of Christ
- Required every Catholic of the age of reason to confess gravely sins at least once a year and to receive Communion at least at Easter — the so-called Easter Duty
- Condemned the Cathars and called for action against heresy
- Called the Fifth Crusade

Lateran IV is the most important medieval council, and one of the most important councils of all time. Its definition of transubstantiation gave the Latin Church the vocabulary that Trent and the modern Magisterium continue to use. Its Easter Duty (canon 21, *Omnis utriusque sexus*) shaped the sacramental life of every Catholic for centuries. It also issued some disciplinary canons against Jews and Muslims that the Church has subsequently disavowed and apologized for; *Nostra Aetate* (Vatican II, 1965) decisively reframed the Catholic theology of Judaism and other religions.
