# The Apostles: Reference for the Catholic Advisor Skill

This reference supports the `catholic-advisor` skill. It collects the twelve apostles called by Christ, plus Matthias who replaced Judas (Acts 1), plus Paul of Tarsus, whom the Church universally numbers among the Apostles though he was not one of the original Twelve. Each entry distinguishes carefully between what Scripture records and what comes from later tradition. Scripture is cited from the New Revised Standard Version Catholic Edition (NRSVCE) on Bible Gateway.

## What an Apostle Is

The word *apostle* means one who is sent. In the New Testament it bears a precise sense: an apostle is one personally called and commissioned by the risen Christ to be a primary witness of the Resurrection and a foundation of the Church (cf. Acts 1:21–22; 1 Corinthians 15:3–8). The Twelve, called by Jesus during his public ministry (Mark 3:13–19), are the first apostles in this sense. Matthias was chosen by lot to restore the number twelve after the defection of Judas (Acts 1:15–26). Paul was called on the road to Damascus and explicitly defended his apostolic credentials by appealing to the appearance of the risen Christ (1 Corinthians 9:1; 15:8).

The Catechism teaches that the Lord made Simon Peter alone the rock of his Church and gave him the keys of the kingdom (CCC 881), and that the bishops are by divine institution the successors of the apostles, the pope as successor of Peter being the perpetual and visible source and foundation of the unity both of the bishops and of the whole company of the faithful (CCC 857–862). The apostolic college is therefore not merely a historical foundation but the abiding structure of the Church in every generation.

Each entry contains feast day, traditional death account, iconographic symbol, what the apostle is principally known for, a narrative summary, and verified Scripture citations.

## The Twelve

The Synoptic Gospels and Acts each give a list of the Twelve (Matthew 10:2–4; Mark 3:16–19; Luke 6:14–16; Acts 1:13). The lists agree on the names with minor variations in order.
