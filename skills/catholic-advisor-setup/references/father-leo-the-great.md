# Saint Leo the Great (c. 400 – 461)

### Saint Leo the Great (c. 400 – 461)

**Era:** Latin (also Pope) | **Feast day:** November 10 | **Age at death:** approximately 61
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1754 by Benedict XIV)
**Major works:** the *Tome of Leo* (Letter 28 to Flavian of Constantinople, the doctrinal foundation of the Council of Chalcedon); ninety-six surviving sermons; over 150 surviving letters
**Known for:** Pope (440–461); the principal Christological author of the Council of Chalcedon; the pope who stopped Attila the Hun in 452

A short summary of his patristic significance only; see also `popes.md`. Leo's Tome to Flavian — sent to the Council of Chalcedon in 451 and acclaimed by the bishops with the cry "Peter has spoken through Leo!" — defines the orthodox Christology that all subsequent Catholic theology presupposes: one Person, two natures, each acting according to its own properties, without confusion. His ninety-six sermons are models of the Roman style: brief, austere, theologically dense. His political acts — most famously the embassy that turned Attila aside from Rome in 452 — illustrate the elevated public role of the Roman bishop in the dying Western Empire.
