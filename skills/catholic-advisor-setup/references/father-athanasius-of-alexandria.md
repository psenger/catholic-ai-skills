# Saint Athanasius of Alexandria (c. 296 – 373)

### Saint Athanasius of Alexandria (c. 296 – 373)

**Era:** Greek | **Feast day:** May 2 | **Age at death:** approximately 77
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1568 by Pius V, among the first four Eastern Doctors)
**Major works:** *On the Incarnation*; *Discourses Against the Arians* (four books); *Life of Antony*
**Known for:** *Athanasius contra mundum* — Athanasius against the world; the indomitable defender of Nicene orthodoxy through five exiles

Athanasius attended the Council of Nicaea (325) as a young deacon and stood with Bishop Alexander against the Arians. Soon afterward he succeeded Alexander as bishop of Alexandria. For the next forty-five years he held the line against successive Arian or semi-Arian emperors and was driven into exile five times. His brief *On the Incarnation* — written when he was perhaps twenty — remains one of the great Christological essays in the patristic corpus: "He was made man that we might be made God" (*On the Incarnation* 54), the classical statement of *theosis* or *divinization*. His *Life of Antony* — the biography of Antony of the Desert — was a sensation in the late ancient world and the prototype of monastic literature. It was the *Life* that Augustine read shortly before his conversion (Confessions VIII.6).
