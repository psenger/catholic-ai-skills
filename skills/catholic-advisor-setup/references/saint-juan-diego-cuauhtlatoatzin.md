# Saint Juan Diego Cuauhtlatoatzin (1474 – 1548)

### Saint Juan Diego Cuauhtlatoatzin (1474 – 1548)

**Feast day:** December 9 | **Age at death:** 74
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2002 by John Paul II — the first indigenous saint of the Americas)
**Known for:** the Aztec/Nahua convert to whom the Virgin Mary appeared at Tepeyac in December 1531, leaving her image on his tilma — the image known as Our Lady of Guadalupe

Juan Diego was a married Nahua peasant of the Tepeyac area near Mexico City, baptized into the Catholic faith by Franciscan missionaries in his late forties. In December 1531 the Virgin appeared to him on Tepeyac hill, spoke to him in Nahuatl, and asked him to request the bishop to build a shrine on that hill. The bishop asked for a sign. The Virgin sent Juan Diego to gather roses out of season; when he opened his tilma to give them to the bishop, the image of the Virgin was found imprinted on the cloth. That image — known as Our Lady of Guadalupe — remains today in the Basilica of Guadalupe in Mexico City and is the most visited Marian shrine in the world. The conversion of millions of indigenous Mexicans in the years that followed is closely tied to this image. The historical and scientific examination of the tilma continues to yield findings that defy ordinary explanation.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
