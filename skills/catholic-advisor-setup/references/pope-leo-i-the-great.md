# Saint Leo I "the Great" (c. 400 – 461) — Pope 440–461

### Saint Leo I "the Great" (c. 400 – 461) — Pope 440–461

**Birth name:** Leo | **Feast day:** November 10 | **Age at death:** approximately 61
**Canonization status:** Saint; Doctor of the Church (proclaimed 1754)
**Known for:** the Tome of Leo at Chalcedon (451); turning aside Attila the Hun from Rome (452); negotiating with the Vandals to spare Rome's people from massacre (455)

Leo is the first pope to whom the title "the Great" has been universally applied. He wrote the *Tome to Flavian* (Letter 28), which the Council of Chalcedon adopted as the standard of orthodox Christology: "Peter has spoken through Leo," the bishops cried. He elevated the Roman primacy in theology and in practice, asserting and exercising appellate authority over the bishops of the Western Church. In 452 he led the embassy that turned Attila aside from Rome; in 455 he met Genseric the Vandal at the gates and persuaded him at least to spare the populace. See also `church-fathers.md`.
