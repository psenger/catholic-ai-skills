# Saint Thomas (d. c. 72)

### Saint Thomas (d. c. 72)

**Feast day:** July 3 | **Age at death:** unknown | **Martyrdom:** pierced by a spear at Mylapore (modern Chennai) in India (strong and ancient Indian tradition, July 3, AD 72)
**Symbol in iconography:** a builder's square (he is the patron of architects, builders, and India); a spear
**Known for:** "My Lord and my God" — his confession after touching the wounds of the risen Christ; apostle to India

Thomas — called *Didymus*, "the Twin" — is best known for the episode after the Resurrection in which he refused to believe unless he could see and touch the wounds of the risen Christ (John 20:24–25). A week later, the Lord appeared again and offered him exactly what he had demanded: "Put your finger here and see my hands. Reach out your hand and put it in my side. Do not doubt but believe." Thomas's reply is the highest Christological confession in the Gospels: "My Lord and my God!" (John 20:28). Jesus answered: "Have you believed because you have seen me? Blessed are those who have not seen and yet have come to believe" (John 20:29). Thomas had earlier shown great courage when Jesus resolved to return to Judea to raise Lazarus, despite the threats against his life: "Let us also go, that we may die with him" (John 11:16). The Indian Church (the Mar Thoma Christians of Kerala) traces its foundation directly to his mission. The witness of this ancient tradition, attested in the *Acts of Thomas* (third century) and in continuous local memory, is among the strongest non-biblical apostolic traditions.
