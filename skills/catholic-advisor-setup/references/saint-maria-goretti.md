# Saint Maria Goretti (1890 – 1902)

### Saint Maria Goretti (1890 – 1902)

**Feast day:** July 6 | **Age at death:** 11
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1950 by Pius XII as the youngest canonized saint in modern times)
**Known for:** the eleven-year-old girl from the marshlands south of Rome who was stabbed to death by a young neighbor attempting to assault her, and who forgave him before she died

Maria was the daughter of a poor family that shared a house with a widowed man and his son Alessandro Serenelli. On July 5, 1902, when Alessandro (eighteen) attempted to assault her, she resisted and told him it was a mortal sin. He stabbed her fourteen times and fled. She survived twenty hours. Asked whether she forgave him, she said: "Yes, for the love of Jesus I forgive him, and I want him to be with me in paradise." Alessandro was sentenced to thirty years. After about eight years in prison he had a dream in which Maria appeared offering him white lilies that became flames in his hands; he converted and spent the rest of his life as a Franciscan tertiary, dying in 1970. He was present in Saint Peter's Square when Pius XII canonized Maria in 1950 alongside her mother, Assunta — the only time a saint's mother has attended her daughter's canonization. Assunta said to the crowd, looking at Alessandro: "Alessandro is our brother."

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
