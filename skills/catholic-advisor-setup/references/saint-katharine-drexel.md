# Saint Katharine Drexel (1858 – 1955)

### Saint Katharine Drexel (1858 – 1955)

**Feast day:** March 3 | **Age at death:** 96
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 2000 by John Paul II)
**Known for:** heiress to a Philadelphia banking fortune who gave it all away; founder of the Sisters of the Blessed Sacrament for Indians and Colored People (1891); educator of Native Americans and African Americans in the segregated United States; founder of Xavier University of Louisiana (1925, the first Catholic historically Black university)

Katharine Drexel was heir to one of the great fortunes of nineteenth-century America. On a trip to Rome in 1887, she asked Pope Leo XIII to send missionaries to Native American and African American communities in the United States; he answered, "Why not become a missionary yourself, my child?" She did. She used her family inheritance — eventually some twenty million dollars — to found schools and missions across the American South and West for Native and Black students, in the teeth of segregation. Her Sisters of the Blessed Sacrament continued the work. She lived to ninety-six and was canonized in 2000.

**Sources:** Vatican.va canonization homily
