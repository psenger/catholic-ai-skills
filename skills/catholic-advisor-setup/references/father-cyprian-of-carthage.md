# Saint Cyprian of Carthage (c. 210 – 258)

### Saint Cyprian of Carthage (c. 210 – 258)

**Era:** Latin | **Feast day:** September 16 (with Cornelius) | **Age at death:** approximately 48
**Doctor of the Church:** No (but venerated as a Father and martyr of the first rank)
**Major works:** *On the Unity of the Catholic Church*; *On the Lord's Prayer*; *On the Lapsed*; eighty-one letters
**Known for:** bishop of Carthage; the great patristic theologian of ecclesial unity; the principal pastoral guide of the Church during the Decian persecution; martyred by beheading under Valerian

Cyprian was a wealthy rhetorician converted around 246; within four years he was bishop of Carthage. His episcopate coincided with two persecutions — the Decian (250) and the Valerianic (257) — and with the resulting pastoral crisis of the *lapsi*, Christians who had publicly sacrificed to the gods under duress and now sought reconciliation. Cyprian's pastoral position — neither lax (immediate readmission) nor rigorist (no readmission) — became the standard Catholic approach: serious penance, then sacramental reconciliation through the bishop. His treatise *On the Unity of the Catholic Church* is the classical patristic statement of the principle: outside the Church there is no salvation; the Church's unity is bound to the unity of the episcopate; and "he cannot have God for his Father who does not have the Church for his Mother" (*De Unitate* 6). He was beheaded near Carthage in 258 under Valerian's order against Christian leaders.
