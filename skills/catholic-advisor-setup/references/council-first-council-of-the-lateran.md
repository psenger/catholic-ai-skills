# 9. First Council of the Lateran (1123)

### 9. First Council of the Lateran (1123)

**Convoked by:** Pope Callixtus II
**Issue addressed:** ratification of the Concordat of Worms (1122), which ended the Investiture Controversy between popes and emperors over the appointment of bishops
**Key teachings:**
- Reaffirmed the principle that the appointment of bishops is a spiritual act reserved to the Church
- Disciplinary canons against simony and clerical marriage

Lateran I is the first ecumenical council held in the West. It came at the close of the Gregorian Reform — the great eleventh-century reform movement, named for Pope Gregory VII (Hildebrand), that fought to free the Church from imperial control over ecclesiastical appointments. The Concordat of Worms divided the bishop's office into its spiritual and temporal aspects: the emperor invested the bishop with the regalia (lands and feudal rights), the pope with the spiritualia (the staff and ring symbolizing the pastoral office). The compromise lasted, and the principle that bishops are made by the Church, not by the state, has been a Catholic non-negotiable since.
