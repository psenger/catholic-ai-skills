# Saint Isidore of Seville (c. 560 – 636)

### Saint Isidore of Seville (c. 560 – 636)

**Era:** Latin (post-patristic in some reckonings, last Father in others) | **Feast day:** April 4 | **Age at death:** approximately 76
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1722 by Innocent XIII)
**Major works:** *Etymologiae* (an encyclopedic compilation in twenty books); *Sententiae*; *History of the Goths*
**Known for:** "the last of the ancients" — the last great mind of the patristic Latin tradition; archbishop of Seville; presided over the Fourth Council of Toledo (633); informal patron of the internet (because his *Etymologies* was a comprehensive cross-referenced compendium of all knowledge — proposed by some bishops, never formally declared)

Isidore inherited the patristic patrimony at a time when the Roman Empire in the West was a memory and the new Visigothic kingdoms were taking shape. His *Etymologiae* was the encyclopedia of the early Middle Ages — copied, abridged, and consulted everywhere from Spain to Ireland to Italy for the next thousand years. He labored to convert the Visigoths from Arianism to Catholic faith and to provide the clergy with the learning needed to lead a Christian society after the collapse of the imperial schools. With him, the strict patristic age in the West ends.
