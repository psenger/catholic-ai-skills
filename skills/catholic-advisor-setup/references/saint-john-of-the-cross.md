# Saint John of the Cross (1542 – 1591)

### Saint John of the Cross (1542 – 1591)

**Feast day:** December 14 | **Age at death:** 49
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1926 by Pius XI); *Doctor Mysticus*
**Known for:** the co-reformer (with Teresa of Ávila) of the Carmelites; the *Dark Night of the Soul*; *The Ascent of Mount Carmel*; *The Spiritual Canticle*; *The Living Flame of Love*

John was a Carmelite priest who joined Teresa of Ávila's reform of the Order. The unreformed branch resisted; in 1577 John was kidnapped by his confreres and imprisoned for nine months in a closet-sized cell, where he composed in his head much of what became his greatest poetry. He escaped, continued the reform, and produced the most exact and rigorous body of mystical theology in the Catholic tradition. His *Dark Night of the Soul* describes the purgative process by which God strips the soul of every sensible consolation in order to lead it into the dark, naked union with himself. He is the patron of contemplatives and the principal Catholic voice on apophatic — wordless, imageless — prayer.
