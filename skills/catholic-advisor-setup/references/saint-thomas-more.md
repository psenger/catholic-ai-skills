# Saint Thomas More (1478 – 1535)

### Saint Thomas More (1478 – 1535)

**Feast day:** June 22 | **Age at death:** 57
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1935); declared Patron of Statesmen and Politicians by John Paul II in 2000
**Known for:** Lord Chancellor of England; author of *Utopia* (1516); refused to swear the Oath of Supremacy acknowledging Henry VIII as Supreme Head of the Church of England; beheaded at the Tower of London

More was the leading Catholic humanist of England, friend of Erasmus and of John Fisher, author of *Utopia*. He served as Lord Chancellor under Henry VIII but resigned the office in 1532 when the king's break with Rome over the divorce from Catherine of Aragon was becoming inevitable. He refused to swear the Oath of Supremacy and was imprisoned in the Tower for over a year. He was tried, convicted of treason on the basis of perjured testimony, and beheaded on July 6, 1535. His last reported words: "I die the king's good servant, but God's first."
