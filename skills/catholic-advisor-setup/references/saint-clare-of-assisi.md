# Saint Clare of Assisi (1194 – 1253)

### Saint Clare of Assisi (1194 – 1253)

**Feast day:** August 11 | **Age at death:** 59
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1255 by Alexander IV)
**Known for:** founder of the Poor Clares (Order of Saint Clare); first woman to write a religious Rule (her Rule was approved by Pope Innocent IV in 1253, two days before her death); the *plant of Francis*

Clare belonged to a noble family of Assisi. At eighteen she fled her family's home in the dead of night, met Francis at the Portiuncula, and was clothed by him in the rough habit of poverty. She lived for forty years at San Damiano just outside Assisi, the heart of the new women's branch of the Franciscan movement. She struggled for decades to preserve absolute Franciscan poverty for her community against papal attempts to give them an endowment — and won the right to do so. The famous incident in which she repulsed a Saracen attack on her monastery by holding up the Eucharist in a monstrance has made her, by association, the patroness of television (since she could, by spiritual vision, "see" events at a distance). She received the privilege of her own Rule — written by her — in 1253.
