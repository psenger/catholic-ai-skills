# Saint Gregory the Great (c. 540 – 604)

### Saint Gregory the Great (c. 540 – 604)

**Era:** Latin (also Pope) | **Feast day:** September 3 (formerly March 12) | **Age at death:** approximately 64
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1295 by Boniface VIII as one of the four original Latin Doctors)
**Major works:** *Pastoral Rule* (the manual of the Christian bishop's office); *Moralia in Job* (the great moral commentary on the Book of Job); *Dialogues* (including the *Life of Benedict*); homilies on Ezekiel and the Gospels; vast correspondence
**Known for:** Pope (590–604); the founder of the medieval papacy; the architect of the conversion of the English; the originator of much of what would become the medieval liturgical and chant tradition (Gregorian chant bears his name)

A short summary of his patristic significance only; see also `popes.md`. Gregory was a Roman aristocrat who became a monk, then a deacon and papal apocrisiary in Constantinople, then reluctantly Pope on the death of Pelagius II. His *Pastoral Rule* — the *Regula Pastoralis* — became the manual for bishops for a thousand years; Alfred the Great translated it into Old English. His *Moralia in Job* is the largest sustained scriptural commentary of the patristic age. He sent Augustine of Canterbury and forty monks to evangelize England in 596 — the mission that established the Christian English Church. With Gregory the patristic age in the West formally closes; he is the last Latin Father in the strict classical sense.
