# Saint Pius X (1835 – 1914) — Pope 1903–1914

### Saint Pius X (1835 – 1914) — Pope 1903–1914

**Birth name:** Giuseppe Sarto | **Feast day:** August 21 | **Age at death:** 79
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1954)
**Known for:** *Pascendi Dominici Gregis* (1907) against Modernism; the lowering of the age of First Communion to the age of reason (1910); the reform of the breviary; the promotion of frequent and daily Communion (1905)

Pius X was a parish priest, bishop, and patriarch of Venice before becoming pope; his pastoral instincts shaped his entire pontificate. His decree *Quam Singulari* (1910) lowered the age of First Communion from twelve to about seven (the age of reason), reversing centuries of practice in many places. His decree *Sacra Tridentina* (1905) urged frequent and even daily Communion among the laity — at a time when annual Communion was the practical norm in many parishes. His campaign against "Modernism" — a complex of theological tendencies tending to reduce dogma to subjective experience — was the principal doctrinal action of his reign. He was canonized in 1954.
