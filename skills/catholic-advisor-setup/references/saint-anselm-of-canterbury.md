# Saint Anselm of Canterbury (1033 – 1109)

### Saint Anselm of Canterbury (1033 – 1109)

**Feast day:** April 21 | **Age at death:** 76
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1720 by Clement XI)
**Known for:** the ontological argument for the existence of God (*Proslogion*, 1078); *Cur Deus Homo* (Why God Became Man); archbishop of Canterbury

Anselm was a Benedictine monk of Bec in Normandy, then abbot, then archbishop of Canterbury. His *Proslogion* contains the most famous philosophical argument for the existence of God: the argument that God, defined as "that than which nothing greater can be conceived," cannot exist only in the mind but must exist in reality, since existence in reality is greater than existence in the mind alone. *Cur Deus Homo* worked out the most influential medieval theory of the Atonement — Christ's death as satisfaction offered to the Father for the offense of human sin. Anselm's motto, *fides quaerens intellectum* ("faith seeking understanding"), became the program of medieval scholastic theology.
