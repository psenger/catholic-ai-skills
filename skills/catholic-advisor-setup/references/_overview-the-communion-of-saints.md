# The Saints and Martyrs: Reference for the Catholic Advisor Skill

This reference supports the `catholic-advisor` skill. It collects roughly seventy major Catholic saints and martyrs — selected to cover the Doctors of the Church (those who are not also Church Fathers, treated separately), the great early martyrs, the founders of religious orders, the modern canonized saints, and the most widely venerated national and group patrons. Each entry has been cross-checked against the *Catholic Encyclopedia* (newadvent.org), vatican.va canonization documents, and other Catholic sources.

**Note on companion files.** The Twelve Apostles, Paul, and Matthias are treated in `apostles.md`. The Church Fathers and Patristic-era Doctors are treated in `church-fathers.md`. The popes — including saint popes — are treated in `popes.md`. This file contains the remaining major saints.

## How the Skill Should Use This File

When a question concerns a particular saint, the universal Roman calendar, a charism associated with a religious order, the historical character of a martyrdom, the development of Catholic devotion, or the example of holiness lived in a particular state of life, consult this file. The *Roman Martyrology* (Holy See, current edition 2004) is the authoritative source for feast days and the official Catholic record of the saints.

**On historical uncertainty.** A number of the early martyrs (especially George, Christopher, Catherine of Alexandria, Cecilia, Philomena, Sebastian, Blaise) have ancient and well-established cults, but the surviving accounts of their lives blend history and pious legend. Where appropriate, the entries below say so explicitly. The Church's veneration of these figures is not invalidated by historical uncertainty about the details of their lives.
