# Saint Louise de Marillac (1591 – 1660)

### Saint Louise de Marillac (1591 – 1660)

**Feast day:** May 9 | **Age at death:** 68
**Canonization status:** Saint (canonized 1934)
**Known for:** co-founder, with Vincent de Paul, of the Daughters of Charity (1633); the first community of women to do nursing and social work outside the cloister

Louise was a young widow when she came under Vincent de Paul's spiritual direction. Together they organized country girls into a new kind of religious community — Daughters of Charity — who would not be cloistered but would live among the poor, serving in hospitals, schools, and homes. The form was canonically novel; their habit was the working dress of the French peasant woman. By the time of Louise's death the community numbered hundreds of sisters; today it is one of the largest active women's congregations in the Catholic Church.

**Sources:** Catholic Encyclopedia entry on Daughters of Charity
