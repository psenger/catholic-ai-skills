# Venerable Pius XII (1876 – 1958) — Pope 1939–1958

### Venerable Pius XII (1876 – 1958) — Pope 1939–1958

**Birth name:** Eugenio Pacelli | **Age at death:** 82
**Canonization status:** Venerable (cause of canonization advanced under Benedict XVI in 2009)
**Known for:** leadership of the Catholic Church through the Second World War; the dogmatic definition of the Assumption of the Blessed Virgin Mary into heaven (*Munificentissimus Deus*, 1950); the encyclicals *Divino Afflante Spiritu* (1943) opening Catholic biblical scholarship to historical-critical methods, *Mystici Corporis Christi* (1943) on the Church as the Mystical Body of Christ, and *Mediator Dei* (1947) on the liturgy

Pius XII's wartime record has been the subject of intense historical debate. The Vatican Secret Archives for the period of his pontificate were opened in 2020, allowing scholars to study his documented actions in detail. The current state of scholarly assessment is that Pius XII worked vigorously and quietly to save Jewish lives — Vatican estimates and independent Jewish historians put the number of Jews saved through Vatican-coordinated networks at over 800,000 — while publicly declining direct condemnation of Nazi Germany on the prudential ground that doing so would worsen the situation for Jews and Catholics in occupied lands. The historiography continues. His doctrinal acts — especially the 1950 definition of the Assumption — are foundational for modern Catholic Mariology. *Divino Afflante Spiritu* (1943) opened the Catholic biblical movement that would flower at Vatican II.
