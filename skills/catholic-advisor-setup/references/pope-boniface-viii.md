# Boniface VIII (c. 1235 – 1303) — Pope 1294–1303

### Boniface VIII (c. 1235 – 1303) — Pope 1294–1303

**Birth name:** Benedetto Caetani | **Age at death:** approximately 68
**Canonization status:** not canonized
**Known for:** the bull *Unam Sanctam* (1302), the strongest claim of papal authority over temporal power in the medieval period; the first Holy Year (1300); the brutal conflict with King Philip IV of France

*Unam Sanctam* declared that "it is altogether necessary to salvation for every human creature to be subject to the Roman Pontiff." The bull is dogmatically authoritative on the necessity of communion with the Roman See, though its full political reading — that the pope has direct temporal jurisdiction over all kings — is not received by the modern Magisterium. Boniface was attacked in his summer palace at Anagni in 1303 by agents of Philip IV and the Colonna family — the "outrage of Anagni" — and died a month later. His pontificate marks the close of the medieval high papal era.
