# Blessed Urban II (c. 1042 – 1099) — Pope 1088–1099

### Blessed Urban II (c. 1042 – 1099) — Pope 1088–1099

**Birth name:** Odo of Châtillon | **Feast day:** July 29 | **Age at death:** approximately 57
**Canonization status:** Blessed (beatified 1881)
**Known for:** calling the First Crusade at the Council of Clermont (November 1095)

Urban responded to the appeal of the Byzantine Emperor Alexius Comnenus for help against the Seljuk Turks. His sermon at Clermont — *Deus vult!* "God wills it!" — set in motion the Crusader movement that would shape European-Levantine relations for two centuries. The First Crusade captured Jerusalem in July 1099; Urban died two weeks later, before the news could reach Rome. The historical and moral assessment of the Crusades remains complex; Pope John Paul II in 2000 offered a public penitential prayer for the wrongs committed by Christians during the Crusades.
