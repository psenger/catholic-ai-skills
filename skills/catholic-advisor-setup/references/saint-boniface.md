# Saint Boniface (c. 675 – 754)

### Saint Boniface (c. 675 – 754)

**Feast day:** June 5 | **Age at death:** approximately 79
**Canonization status:** Saint (pre-congregation)
**Known for:** the apostle of Germany; reformer of the Frankish Church; martyred at Dokkum (Frisia) at seventy-nine

Wynfrith — who took the name Boniface — was an Anglo-Saxon Benedictine monk who in 716 left England for the mission field in Frisia and the German tribes. The dramatic moment of his mission came in 723 when, at Geismar in Hesse, he cut down the great oak of Thor sacred to the local pagans. The tree fell; nothing happened to him; the pagans converted. He went on to organize the Church of central Germany, founded the great monastery of Fulda, and at seventy-nine returned to the Frisian mission, where he was murdered by raiders at Dokkum on June 5, 754. He was reading from a Gospel book; the cut-marks on it survive.
