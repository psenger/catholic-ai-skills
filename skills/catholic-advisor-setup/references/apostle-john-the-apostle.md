# Saint John the Apostle (d. c. 100)

### Saint John the Apostle (d. c. 100)

**Feast day:** December 27 | **Age at death:** approximately 90+ (tradition) | **Martyrdom:** the only apostle traditionally not martyred — said to have died of natural causes in extreme old age at Ephesus; he was, however, exiled to Patmos
**Symbol in iconography:** eagle (his Gospel "soars" theologically); chalice with a serpent (he was reportedly given poisoned wine that did not harm him)
**Known for:** the Beloved Disciple; the Fourth Gospel, three Letters, and the Apocalypse; the apostle entrusted with the care of the Virgin Mary

John, son of Zebedee and brother of James the Greater, called with his brother (Matthew 4:21–22). The Fourth Gospel identifies its source as "the disciple whom Jesus loved" — the Beloved Disciple — who leaned on the Lord's breast at the Last Supper (John 13:23), stood at the foot of the cross and received the dying command "Behold your mother" (John 19:26–27), thereafter taking the Virgin Mary into his own home, ran to the empty tomb with Peter on Easter morning (John 20:2–8), and recognized the risen Lord first on the shore of Tiberias (John 21:7). Tradition places his ministry at Ephesus, his exile to Patmos under Domitian (where he received the visions recorded in the Apocalypse, Revelation 1:9), and his peaceful death in great age. Three Letters are attributed to him; the Fourth Gospel is the deepest theological reflection on the Incarnation in the New Testament canon.
