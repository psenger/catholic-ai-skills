# Saint Albert the Great (c. 1200 – 1280)

### Saint Albert the Great (c. 1200 – 1280)

**Feast day:** November 15 | **Age at death:** approximately 80
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1931 by Pius XI); *Doctor Universalis*
**Known for:** the teacher of Thomas Aquinas; one of the greatest natural scientists of the Middle Ages; bishop of Regensburg

Albert was a Dominican who taught at Paris and Cologne. His student Thomas Aquinas would eclipse him, but Albert himself was a polymath of staggering range: theology, philosophy, biology, chemistry (alchemy in its day), botany, mineralogy. He is the patron saint of natural scientists. When the bones of Aquinas were exhumed and his canonization proceedings advanced, Albert is reported (by tradition) to have wept and to have said that his student had been better than he. He died at eighty in Cologne.
