# 8. Fourth Council of Constantinople (869–870)

### 8. Fourth Council of Constantinople (869–870)

**Convoked by:** Pope Adrian II (under the Emperor Basil I)
**Issue addressed:** the so-called Photian schism in Constantinople
**Key teachings:**
- Deposed Photius and restored Ignatius as Patriarch of Constantinople
- Affirmed the prerogatives of the papacy

This is the last of the Catholic-recognized first-millennium councils. The Eastern Orthodox do not accept it as ecumenical; they prefer the synod of 879–880 that reinstated Photius. The historical and theological status of Photius is contested: Catholic tradition long treated him as the architect of the East-West schism, but more recent scholarship and ecumenical engagement have substantially rehabilitated him. The council itself was largely concerned with disciplinary matters and does not appear in lists of dogmatic landmarks.
