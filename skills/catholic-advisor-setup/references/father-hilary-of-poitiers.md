# Saint Hilary of Poitiers (c. 310 – 367)

### Saint Hilary of Poitiers (c. 310 – 367)

**Era:** Latin | **Feast day:** January 13 | **Age at death:** approximately 57
**Doctor of the Church:** Yes (proclaimed 1851 by Pius IX)
**Major works:** *On the Trinity* (twelve books); *On the Synods*; *Commentary on Matthew*; *Commentary on the Psalms*
**Known for:** *the Athanasius of the West*; the principal Western defender of Nicene orthodoxy against Arianism; bishop of Poitiers

A convert from paganism, Hilary became bishop of Poitiers around 350. The Arian crisis was at its height in the West, and Hilary's refusal to subscribe to a semi-Arian formula imposed by the Emperor Constantius II earned him four years of exile in Phrygia (356–360). The exile turned out to be theological opportunity: cut off from the Latin West and immersed in Eastern theology, Hilary mastered Greek Trinitarian thought and brought it back to the Latin Church. His *On the Trinity* is the first major Latin Trinitarian treatise. He died at Poitiers in 367, having lived to see the tide turn against Arianism.
