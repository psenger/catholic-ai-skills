# Saint Francis of Assisi (1181–1226)

**Feast day:** October 4 | **Beatitude(s):** Poor in spirit, mourning, meek | **Virtue(s):** Poverty of spirit, temperance, charity
**Canonized:** 1228

The son of a wealthy cloth merchant, Francis lived a dissolute and pleasure-seeking young adulthood before a series of experiences — imprisonment in war, illness, and the encounter with a leper — brought about a gradual conversion. The moment he identified as decisive: he was riding and encountered a leper, a sight that normally filled him with disgust. He dismounted, embraced and kissed the man, and rode on. When he looked back, the man was gone. He renounced his inheritance publicly, in front of his father and the bishop of Assisi, stripped himself of his clothes, and returned them to his father. He went on to found the Franciscan order.

**Key quotes:**
> "Start by doing what is necessary; then do what is possible; and suddenly you are doing the impossible." *(attributed — widely cited)*
> "Preach the Gospel at all times. When necessary, use words." *(attributed — commonly cited but likely not his exact words; consistent with his practice)*
> "Lord, make me an instrument of your peace." *(attributed to him but written centuries later — the Peace Prayer is not his text, though it reflects his spirit)*
> From his Canticle of the Creatures: "Be praised, my Lord, through all your creatures." *(documented — one of the earliest texts in the Italian vernacular)*

**The point:** His conversion was not from dramatic sin but from an empty life — from a young man who had everything wealth and status could provide and found it insufficient. His turning point was not a theological argument but a physical act: he touched someone he had been taught to avoid. The argument he makes for teenagers and young adults shaped by achievement culture is that the life they are being formed to want may not be the life they are made for.

**In conversation:** *"Francis of Assisi grew up wealthy. He wanted to be a knight — glory, status, the life everyone told him to want. He got imprisoned in war, got sick, recovered, and then one day came across a leper on the road. His whole life, that sight had disgusted him. This time he got off his horse and embraced the man. He wrote later that it was the moment his whole life changed. He gave back everything he owned to his father. Standing in front of the bishop. In public. He wasn't trying to be dramatic. He had just figured out what mattered."*

**Age group:** Teenagers (15–17), young adults (18–25). Particularly useful for conversations about wealth, status, achievement culture, and vocation.
