# Saint Cecilia (approx. 3rd century)

**Feast day:** November 22 | **Beatitude(s):** Pure in heart, persecuted for righteousness | **Virtue(s):** Fortitude, purity

A Roman noblewoman who, according to tradition, told her husband Valerian on their wedding night that she had consecrated her virginity and was protected by an angel. She asked him to go to the Appian Way and seek Bishop Urban, who baptized him. Her husband and his brother both eventually accepted martyrdom. She was sentenced to death by suffocation, survived, and was beheaded. While the historical details are uncertain, her veneration is documented by the 5th century and she appears on the Roman calendar.

**Key quotes:**
> "I have an angel of God who loves me and guards my body with great jealousy." *(traditional — from early hagiography)*
> "Nothing that I love is hidden from you." *(traditional — to her husband, explaining her faith)*

**The point:** She negotiated; she explained. She did not refuse her husband silently — she told him what she believed and why, and she pointed him toward the faith. Her story is useful for conversations about how a young person communicates their values in a relationship, including when those values are countercultural.

**In conversation:** *"Cecilia was given in an arranged marriage — she had no choice about that. But on the night of her wedding she told her husband what she believed and why it mattered to her. She didn't just shut down; she explained herself. He went and found a bishop, got baptized, and eventually died for the faith. She started the conversation."*

**Age group:** Teenagers (14–17). **Patron:** musicians, composers, singers.
