# Saints Louis and Zelie Martin (1823–1894 and 1831–1877)

**Feast day:** July 12 | **Beatitude(s):** Mourning, pure in heart | **Virtue(s):** Faith, hope
**Canonized together:** 2015

The parents of Saint Thérèse of Lisieux and the first married couple canonized together in modern times. They lost four children in infancy. Zelie died of breast cancer when Thérèse was four years old. Louis later suffered a severe mental breakdown and spent three years in a psychiatric asylum, cared for by his daughters. He died after suffering a series of strokes.

**Key quotes:**
> Zelie: "I love life. But I hold it with a light hand, because I know that eternal life is what matters." *(attributed — drawn from her published letters)*
> Zelie on losing a child: "I am heartbroken, but I bow my head before God." *(documented — from her letters)*
> Louis, during his illness: "I always asked God for one grace in my life — to be able to suffer for Him. He has granted it." *(documented — reported by his daughters)*

**The point:** Their canonization is a pastoral statement: holiness is possible within marriage and family life, including marriages marked by infant death, terminal illness, and mental breakdown. They did not have an idealized or protected family life. Their witness is useful for parents who feel their circumstances disqualify them from the faith they want to model for their children.

**In conversation:** *"The parents of Thérèse of Lisieux — both of them — were canonized in 2015. They lost four children as infants. Zelie died of cancer when Thérèse was four. Louis later had a severe mental breakdown and had to be committed to a psychiatric hospital. His daughters cared for him. Both of them are saints. The Church is saying: that life, with all of that in it, was a holy life."*

**Age group:** Young adults (18+), particularly parents in the coaching context.
