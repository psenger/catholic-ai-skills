# Saint Tarcisius (3rd century)

**Feast day:** August 15 | **Beatitude(s):** Persecuted for righteousness | **Virtue(s):** Faith, fortitude
**Age at death:** believed to be an early teenager

A young acolyte in early Christian Rome who carried the Eucharist to imprisoned Christians awaiting execution. He was discovered by a pagan mob and killed rather than surrender the sacrament. His story is known primarily through Pope Damasus I's epitaph for him, which compares him to Stephen, the first martyr. No direct quotes survive.

**The point:** He understood what he was carrying and what its loss would mean — and he chose to absorb the cost rather than allow it. For a young person already engaged in the liturgy, his story makes the Eucharist something worth protecting, not merely something to receive.

**In conversation:** *"There was a young altar server in Rome in the third century named Tarcisius. He was carrying Communion to Christians in prison — which was itself illegal at the time. A mob stopped him and demanded he hand it over. He wouldn't. He died protecting the sacrament. No one knows exactly how old he was. Old enough to know what he was carrying. That's all."*

**Age group:** Children (9–11), early teenagers (12–14). He is the patron of altar servers; the liturgical connection is direct.
