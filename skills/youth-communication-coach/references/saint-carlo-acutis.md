# Blessed Carlo Acutis (1991–2006)

**Feast day:** October 12 | **Beatitude(s):** Pure in heart | **Virtue(s):** Faith, charity
**Beatified:** 2020 | **Age at death:** 15

A Milan teenager who coded websites, loved soccer and video games, received daily Communion, and created a worldwide website cataloging Eucharistic miracles. When he was diagnosed with fulminant leukemia at fifteen, he offered his suffering to God for the Pope and for all souls in purgatory. He died within days. His mother said he was the same child on his last day as his first: cheerful, grounded, and entirely himself.

**Key quotes:**
> "The Eucharist is my highway to heaven." *(documented — repeated frequently and recorded by those who knew him)*
> "All people are born as originals, but many die as photocopies." *(attributed)*
> "To always be close to Jesus, that's my life plan." *(documented — from his own writings)*

**The point:** Holiness is not reserved for another century or a different kind of person. He was online, funny, competitive at video games, deeply attached to his friends — and completely serious about his faith at the same time. He did not experience these as opposites. He is the most credible counterargument to the idea that faith is for people who have checked out of ordinary life.

**In conversation:** *"There's a kid from Milan who died in 2006 — fifteen years old, your age. He built websites, played video games, had a social life. He also went to Mass every day and said the Eucharist was his highway to heaven. His name is Carlo Acutis. He was beatified in 2020, which is the Church saying: this was the real thing."*

**Age group:** Children (9–11), teenagers (12–17). The technological detail is essential — it is what makes him credible. Do not omit it.
