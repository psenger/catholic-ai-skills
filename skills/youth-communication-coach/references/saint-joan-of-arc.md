# Saint Joan of Arc (1412–1431)

**Feast day:** May 30 | **Beatitude(s):** Persecuted for righteousness | **Virtue(s):** Fortitude, justice
**Canonized:** 1920 | **Age at death:** approximately 19

An illiterate French peasant girl who heard voices she identified as Saints Michael, Catherine, and Margaret, led the French army to several military victories, and was burned at the stake following a politically motivated ecclesiastical trial. The charges were later overturned. Her tribunal record survives — one of the most completely documented trials in medieval history. When her judges tried to trap her, she answered with precision and composure. When asked whether she was in a state of grace, she said: if she was, God would keep her in it; if she was not, God would bring her to it. The judges later said they had never heard a better answer.

**Key quotes:**
> "I am not afraid. I was born to do this." *(attributed — widely cited, exact original unclear)*
> "If I am not in a state of grace, may God bring me there; if I am, may God keep me there." *(documented — from trial records, her actual answer to a theological trap)*
> "One life is all we have and we live it as we believe in living it. But to sacrifice what you are and to live without belief, that is a fate more terrible than dying." *(attributed — possibly apocryphal but consistent with the trial record)*
> "Act, and God will act." *(attributed)*

**The point:** She stood before an ecclesiastical court that had already decided its verdict, and she held her position — not out of stubbornness but out of clarity about what she actually experienced. She did not perform certainty she did not have; her tribunal answer about grace is a model of honest humility. Her case also opens honest discussion about institutional failure within the Church, handled well: the same Church that condemned her later overturned the verdict and canonized her.

**In conversation:** *"Joan of Arc was nineteen when she was burned at the stake, by a Church tribunal. The Church later overturned the verdict and made her a saint. In between, she stood in front of judges who were trying to trap her. One of them asked her: are you in a state of grace? — which was a trick, because either answer could be used against her. She said: 'If I am, God keep me there; if I'm not, God bring me there.' They said they'd never heard a better answer. Nineteen years old."*

**Age group:** Teenagers (13–17), young adults (18+). Her case is particularly useful when a young person is under institutional pressure, or when the topic of the Church's failures comes up honestly in a coaching conversation.
