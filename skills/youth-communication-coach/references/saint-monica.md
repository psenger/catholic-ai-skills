# Saint Monica (332–387)

**Feast day:** August 27 | **Beatitude(s):** Mourning, meek | **Virtue(s):** Hope, charity as patient love

Mother of Augustine, Monica prayed for his conversion for approximately seventeen years. She followed him secretly to Rome when he fled to Italy to escape her. He was baptized by Ambrose in 387, a few weeks after which Monica died in Ostia, at peace, saying she had nothing more she wanted from this world. Augustine's *Confessions* includes a long and beautiful account of their final conversation, conducted as a shared contemplation of heaven.

**Key quotes:**
> "Nothing is far from God." *(documented — her words as she died, from Augustine's Confessions, Book IX)*
> "Only this I ask: that you remember me at the altar of God, wherever you may be." *(documented — Augustine's Confessions, her request before death)*
> Ambrose to Augustine about his mother: "It is impossible that the son of so many tears should perish." *(documented — Augustine's Confessions)*

**The point:** She did not manage Augustine's conversion — she could not. She prayed, stayed in relationship, followed him, and waited. The argument she makes is not strategic; it is theological: love that stays in relationship without control is itself a form of prayer. This is the model for parents whose children have wandered from faith: not argument, not ultimatums, but faithful presence and intercession.

**In conversation:** *"Monica prayed for her son Augustine for seventeen years. She followed him to a different country when he ran. She went to her bishop in tears, and he told her: 'The son of so many tears cannot perish.' He was right, but she didn't know that yet. For seventeen years she didn't know. She just kept praying. Augustine later wrote one of the greatest accounts of a mother and son in the history of literature. He said she was the one who made him."*

**Age group:** Primarily useful for parents in the coaching context. All adult age groups.
