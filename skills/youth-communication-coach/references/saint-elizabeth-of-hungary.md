# Saint Elizabeth of Hungary (1207–1231)

**Feast day:** November 17 | **Beatitude(s):** Poor in spirit, merciful | **Virtue(s):** Charity, justice
**Canonized:** 1235 | **Age at death:** 24

A Hungarian princess, daughter of King Andrew II, married at fourteen and widowed at twenty. Despite being royalty, she built hospitals, sold her jewelry to feed the poor, and was known to carry the sick in her own arms. After her husband's death, her in-laws expelled her from the castle with her children. She joined the Third Order of Francis, lived in poverty, and died at twenty-four, having given away everything she had.

**Key quotes:**
> "How could I stand before the Lord with my royal jewels, when He Himself was crowned with thorns?" *(attributed)*
> "We must give God what we have, however little." *(attributed)*
> "I could no longer bear to see the poor suffer." *(attributed — reported by contemporaries)*

**The point:** Wealth and status are not obstacles to holiness but they are, in her case, things she specifically had to surrender to reach it. She is useful not only as a figure of charity but as an example of someone who chose downward mobility — from princess to Third Order Franciscan — with full awareness of what she was choosing.

**In conversation:** *"Elizabeth of Hungary was a princess who kept giving things away. She gave away her jewels. She gave away her time. She built hospitals and personally cared for people in them. When her husband died, her in-laws threw her and her children out of the castle. She was twenty years old. She ended up in a small house with a few companions, doing the same work. She died at twenty-four. The Church canonized her two years later."*

**Age group:** Teenagers (15–17), young adults (18–25). Useful in conversations about wealth, privilege, vocation, and what it means to actually do something with your life.
